The cardinal landed on his mirror fifteen days after his father died. This book is what it took to understand why.

Rob Brizzi was four years old when his mother carried him out of Mexico City to start over with nothing. He was thirty when his adoptive father, Lou, rented a U-Haul and drove to West Palm Beach to bring home a son who was dying by inches. And he was seventeen years sober, sitting in a parking lot between hospice visits, when a red bird landed on his driver’s-side mirror and refused to leave.

The Cardinal’s Promise is a memoir of addiction and recovery, of two fathers — one who kept calling and one who kept coming — and of the work Rob does now: sitting with families in the last chapter, showing up the way Lou taught him. It is a book about the love that doesn’t send a bill.
