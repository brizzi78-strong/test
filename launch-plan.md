## The thesis

The books are not the product. **Trust is the product.** The flywheel:

```
Talks + free guide  →  recognition as THE local end-of-life educator
        →  book sales (back-of-room at 3x Amazon margin)
        →  workbook sales (individual + institutional bulk)
        →  speaking fees
        →  hospice referral channel  (claimed: +50% ≈ $30K/yr — see gate #3)
```

Pure book-retail economics are negative (break-even ~2,500 copies vs. a
median self-pub lifetime of <1,000). The venture is justified by the
speaking + hospice channels. Every gate below exists to keep that honest.

## Gates & kill criteria

1. **Gigs before launch.** Book **3 speaking commitments** (paid or
   unpaid: churches, FPU groups, senior communities, employer
   lunch-and-learns) *before* the adult book ships. If the calendar
   can't fill without the book, the book won't fix it.
2. **Kids book validation — costs $0, do first.** Ask 5 children's
   bereavement coordinators + 3 funeral homes: *"If a cardinal-themed
   picture book existed for a child grieving the stepfather who chose
   them, would you buy 25 copies?"* **3+ yeses → fund the illustrator.
   Blank stares → $6K saved.** Lead the calls with the window cover and
   the positioning line: stepparent-loss books barely exist; blended
   families are ~40% of US families.
3. **The hospice thesis must be falsifiable.** Within 12 months of
   launch, write down and track: X talks delivered to referral-source
   audiences, Y new referral relationships, Z change in comp. The
   "+50% / $30K" claim is the ROI case — it gets measured, not assumed.
4. **Kill criterion.** If 6 months post-launch there are <3 paid gigs
   booked: ads stop, spending stops, the books become long-tail assets,
   and the thesis gets re-examined. No new spend without a new gate.

## Kids book — creative decisions (locked)

- **Characters:** Rob and Lou — the true story. The boy is Rob ("that
  boy was me" from the stage). In dialogue he's "Grandpa"; the
  narration names him Lou. *Not* "Sam" — distance is the one thing this
  book must not have.
- **The story:** Lou is the stepfather who chose him. Structure: porch
  (Rob and Mom, before) → **the wedding (the choosing)** → pool, soccer,
  the baseball glove (the becoming) → the quiet page → the winter
  window → the cardinal. Lou ages across the pages; the promise was
  made freely — that's what makes it land.
- **Subtitle:** "A Story About Love That Stays." Locked.
- **Cover composition:** boy + Lou at the snowy window, cardinal
  foreground on the sill. Locked.
- **Art:** concept art (AI) is the *brief*, not the final product.
  Human illustrator, one character model, painterly warmth preferred.
  Three reasons: 32-page character consistency; KDP's AI-content
  disclosure (institutional buyers check); copyright ownership of Rob
  and Lou's faces. This is the one product where the art *is* the product.
- **Dedication:** *"For Lou — whose name I kept."* (Robert Louis Brizzi.)

1. **Institutional bulk** — hospices, bereavement programs, grief camps,
   senior-living communities, funeral homes buying 25–200 units
   (workbook as family-onboarding gift; kids book via children's
   bereavement programs). Needs a one-page sell sheet + bulk tier, $0 ads.

3. **Earned media** — grief expert + author = local TV/radio segments
   (holidays-and-grief runs every year), podcast tour.
4. **Amazon** — discoverability layer. Capped ads, keyworded subtitles.

**Compliance notes:** Resource Guide stays editorially neutral — no paid
placements steering referrals (anti-kickback exposure + credibility).
Community grief education is the cleanest compliant marketing channel in
hospice; keep it that way. Track referral attribution from day one.

## IP protection

**The gap:** purely AI-generated images are not copyrightable (no human
authorship) — the concept art cannot be owned or protected. It stays
**internal brief material only**; the public never sees it as "the art."
The first public images of The Cardinal's Promise are the ones the LLC
legally owns.

**Checklist:**

- [ ] **Illustrator contract with copyright assignment** ("work made for
      hire" / all-rights assignment to Blue Ridge Financial Coaching LLC
      on payment) — covering final art AND the character designs of Rob
      and Lou. One-time attorney review of the template; reuse forever.
      Without this clause the illustrator owns the art by default.
- [ ] **Copyright registration** (copyright.gov, $45–65/work) for each
      finished book — text + final art as a unit. Registration is what
      enables statutory damages ($750–30K/work, $150K willful);
      automatic copyright alone only gets provable actual losses.
- [ ] **Trademark "The Cardinal's Promise" + cardinal mark** as a
      series/brand (USPTO, ~$250–350/class). Titles can't be
      copyrighted; the trademark is what protects the three-product
      brand line. Strongest moat in this list.
- [ ] **Notice everywhere:** `© 2026 Rob Brizzi / Blue Ridge Financial
      Coaching LLC. All rights reserved.` — copyright pages, back of the
      free guide, website footer.
- [ ] **Posting rules:** low-res only (≤1000px), corner credit
      ("© Rob Brizzi · TheCardinalsPromise.com" — markets while it
      protects), never print-resolution files or full interior spreads.
- [ ] **Embed IPTC/EXIF creator + copyright metadata** in every released
      image file.
- [ ] **Keep provenance:** sketches, drafts, contract, payments — the
      paper trail that wins "that's mine."
