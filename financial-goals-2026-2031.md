### 2. The Roth conversion ladder (Years 1–5+) — the centerpiece

The $426K traditional IRA is the household's biggest future tax bill
(RMDs later, ordinary rates). With a 15+ year horizon, convert it to
Roth gradually:

- **Convert ~$60–80K/year**, sized each December to fill (not blow
  through) the 24% federal bracket — exact amount set annually with the
  CPA after W-2s and any business income are known.
- **Pay conversion tax from the cash fund, never from the IRA** — that's
  what makes conversions mathematically strong.
- **Sequencing bonus:** once the traditional IRA is empty (~5–7 years),
  the pro-rata rule dies, and **clean backdoor Roth IRAs** (~$7K+/person
  /year) open up forever. Until then, conversions take priority over
  backdoor contributions.
- Every converted dollar compounds tax-free for 15+ years and never
  faces an RMD. At 7%, $426K converted by ~2032 is **~$1.2M+ of
  tax-free money by 2041**.
