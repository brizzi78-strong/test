# The Promise: A Name to Grow Into — picture book, first-draft text

**Format:** 32-page picture book, ~600 words, ages 4–8
**Position:** the adoption / chosen-family book. Companion to The Little
Cardinal's Promise (the grief book). Matches Rob's cover mock: "THE PROMISE —
A Name to Grow Into," back-cover line "You picked me once. Now I spend my
life picking others."
**Art:** Rob's AI concept art (Lou with glasses + mustache, the boy, the
chihuahua). Keep shirts/signs blank in generated art — add text in layout.
**Note:** no death in this book — the promise stays alive. Grief belongs to
the cardinal book.

---

SPREAD 1 (pages 4–5)
Once there was a boy who came from far away,
with his mama and his little brother
and one small bag between them.
[Art: airplane window, city lights below, boy's face reflected.]

SPREAD 2 (6–7)
Everything here was new. New words. New snow. New school.
The boy felt like a guest everywhere he went —
even at his own lunch table.
[Art: boy small at the end of a crowded cafeteria table.]

SPREAD 3 (8–9)
Then Mama met Lou.
Lou had glasses and a mustache
and a laugh you could hear from the driveway.
[Art: Lou in the doorway mid-laugh, matching Rob's concept art.]

SPREAD 4 (10–11)
Lou didn't have to come to the boy's games.
He came anyway. Third row. Every single time.
[Art: bleachers, Lou third row, arms crossed, watching.]

SPREAD 5 (12–13)
Lou didn't have to hit him fly balls at the park.
He hit a hundred. Then a hundred more.
"One more?" the boy would ask.
"Always," Lou said.
[Art: golden evening, ball high over the field.]

SPREAD 6 (14–15)
One night, when Lou pulled the blanket up to his chin,
the boy asked the question he'd been holding all day.
"Can I call you Dad?"
[Art: dark bedroom, hall light through the cracked door.]

SPREAD 7 (16–17)
Lou's eyes went shiny.
"Yes," he said. "Of course, son."
And he finished the tucking-in like it was any other night —
except now the boy had a word he didn't have before.
[Art: close on both faces. The quietest spread in the book.]

SPREAD 8 (18–19)
Then came the day at the courthouse, in scratchy nice clothes,
when the boy got something else new.
A name. The family's name. His now, forever.
[Art: judge's bench from a kid's-eye view, Mama crying happy.]

SPREAD 9 (20–21)
The new name felt like Dad's jacket —
warm, but way too big.
At school he wrote it slowly, letter by letter,
and it still didn't feel like his.
[Art: pencil, paper, a name half-erased and rewritten.]

SPREAD 10 (22–23)
"I don't fit my name," the boy told his dad.
Dad thought about that the way he thought about everything. Slowly.
"A name isn't something you fit," he said.
"A name is something you grow into. Watch me. I'll show you how."
[Art: the two of them on the porch steps, dog between them.]

SPREAD 11 (24–25)
So the boy watched.
He watched his dad show up in the rain and in the snow,
on good days and on tired ones,
for neighbors and strangers and always — always — for him.
That was the whole lesson. Show up. Stay.
[Art: montage — umbrella, shoveled walk, waiting room, third row.]

SPREAD 12 (26–27)
The boy grew. And year by year,
the name fit a little better.
Like a jacket he was finally tall enough to wear.
[Art: same jacket across three ages — drowning in it, close, perfect fit.]

SPREAD 13 (28–29)
Now the boy is grown.
And when somebody's day is heavy, he pulls up a chair.
When somebody feels like a guest at their own table,
he slides over and says, "Come sit with us."
[Art: grown man in a doorway with a chair, echo of Lou in spread 3.]

SPREAD 14 (30–31)
Because his dad picked him once.
And now he spends his whole life picking others.
[Art: man and old Lou side by side on the porch, dog asleep between.]

SPREAD 15 (32)
That was the promise.
It was kept for him.
And a promise that gets kept... keeps going.
[Art: the porch light on, evening, the name BRIZZI on the mailbox.]

THE END

Back matter (small type): For every family built on purpose — adoptive,
blended, chosen. A note that "grown into" names take time, and that's okay.
