Prelude

The Cardinal

January 9, 2026

Fifteen days after my father died, a red cardinal landed on my car.

It was the seventeenth anniversary of my sobriety. I sat completely still, as if my body recognized something my mind hadn't caught up to yet.

In seventeen years of staying sober — in the long stretch from dying alone in West Palm Beach to building a life shaped by discipline, love, and service — nothing like this had ever happened.

I didn't move. I didn't reach for meaning. It arrived anyway.

This wasn't a coincidence. It was confirmation.

My father — the man who chose to be my dad when biology gave him every excuse not to — was sending a message that didn't need words:

Keep showing up. Keep choosing love. Keep serving.

Only later would I understand how long I'd been receiving that message — how often it had come disguised as people, hard lines, second chances, and quiet routines.

Standing there at the edge of goodbye, one truth settled in me without argument:

Loss doesn't destroy purpose. It reveals it.
