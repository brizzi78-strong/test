Companion to `financial-goals-2026-2031.md`. Governs the ~$330K being
deployed from the Pensacola proceeds plus ongoing monthly investing.
**Principle #1: with ~$580K liquid and a 15+ year horizon, the biggest
risk isn't picking the wrong fund — it's becoming a trader.** This plan
makes the real money boring and gives the itch a sandbox.

## 3. Rules (the IPS — investment policy statement)

1. Rebalance once a year (December, same meeting as the conversion).
2. Never sell equities in a drawdown; the EF exists so we never have to.
3. No position >5% of portfolio outside the index funds.
4. No leverage, no options selling/buying in the core, no crypto beyond
   the existing ~$4.8K.
5. Changes to this plan require 30 days between "I want to" and "I did."

## 4. Paper trading — the sandbox protocol

Purpose: scratch the active-trading itch with zero dollars, and generate
honest evidence before any real money plays.

**Platforms** (all free):
- **thinkorswim paperMoney** (Schwab — you already have Schwab accounts;
  most realistic fills, full options chains)
- **Webull Paper Trading** (simplest to start)
- **Investopedia Simulator** (good for a friendly competition with Hope)
- Note: Robinhood has **no** paper mode — don't practice where you hold
  real money anyway.

**The protocol:**
1. Fund the paper account with exactly **$10K** (the size the real play
   bucket would be — realistic stakes, realistic sizing).
2. Trade for **6 months minimum**. Journal every trade: thesis, entry,
   exit plan, outcome vs. plan.
3. Benchmark honestly against **VTI bought on day 1** (dividends
   included). Not "did I make money" — "did I beat just-buying-the-index."
4. **Gate:** beat VTI over 6 months → fund the real play bucket, $10K
   hard cap, same journal. Lose to VTI → the itch was scratched for
   free, and the index keeps the job.
5. Paper results reset if unused for 6+ months — markets change.

**Honest odds disclosure:** ~90% of active retail traders underperform
the index over time. The protocol isn't there to launch a trading career;
it's there to *prove to yourself* which side of that number you're on
before real dollars find out.
