# Scott's Homework — The Walking List
*Talk-to-text, on a walk, don't overthink. One question at a time. Send me the raw bursts in any order — I'll place every fragment. Scott wants DETAILS and interior: what you were thinking, not what happened.*

## The core assignment: last glass of wine → Chumuckla
*(Scott's exact gap. Also the #1 finding of every review round.)*

1. The night before rehab — Carrabba's, the last glass of wine. What did the wine taste like knowing it was the last one? What were you thinking while Lou sat across from you? Did either of you say anything about what tomorrow was?
2. Rewind two weeks: New Orleans has fallen apart. Walk me through the days between New Orleans and saying yes. Where were you sleeping? What did each day look like?
3. Chris's call — the one before Chumuckla. What did he actually say? What did you say back? Where were you standing?
4. The moment the YES came out of you. Was it a decision, an exhaustion, a surrender, a trick you played on yourself? What did you think rehab was going to be, the night before you went?
5. CORPORATE ROB'S HEAD: you'd been the number-one rep, the president's club guy. What was the story you were still telling yourself about who you were, even at 145 pounds? When did that story finally break?
6. Ignacio's $10,000 — the reunion money that went to pills. What did you tell yourself while you were spending it? When did the shame of that one actually arrive — then, or years later?
7. Guilt inventory, unfiltered: what were the three things you felt guiltiest about in those last weeks of using? Don't rank them politely — what actually woke you up at night?
8. Fear inventory: what were you afraid of about getting clean? (Not the withdrawal — the identity. Who would you be without it?)
9. First 72 hours at Chumuckla: what did your body do? What did your head say? When was the first moment you thought this might work — and the first moment you almost left?
10. The what and the why, plainly: WHAT were you addicted to, in your own words, beyond the pill names — the feeling, the escape, the performance? And WHY do you believe it took you and not, say, Chris?

## While you're walking anyway — the two wounds our reviewers found
11. CHRISTMAS DAY: who called you? Where were you standing? What's the first thing you did? Was Sandra with Lou? Was hospice ever discussed for him — offered, refused, too late? Was he in pain at the end?
12. THE LAST WORDS: the last actual phone call with Lou — what did he say? What did you say?
13. THE WEDNESDAY PHONE: does Ignacio still call? Does he know Lou died? What do you do when the phone rings now?

## Shorter walks — the itch list
