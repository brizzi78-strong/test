# Chapter 16: The Cell

Four years in. Saturday like the others. Same scrubs, same bag, same border.

A different cop sees the watch.

He takes it. Then the bag.

He pulls me out of the car anyway.

It is not a prison — it is a holding cell, a building I could not have found on a map if you put a gun to my head.

The smell hits before the door closes behind me.

Sewer.

Vomit.

Mold.

Dark.

A rat moves across the floor chasing a cockroach.

Men are speaking Spanish faster than I can follow. Some of them are out of their minds, some sitting on the floor like they have been there a long time.

Some of them look at me the way you look at somebody who does not belong.

One of them comes at me.

A small man. Rico. On meth.

His hands on my throat.

"Gringo. Gringo. Money."

I have nothing — no watch, no bag, no wallet.

He keeps asking anyway. I tell him I have nothing. I do not know if he understands me.

After a while he gives up. He sits back down against the opposite wall.

I slide down the wall to the floor.

I put my head on my knees.

It was one night. I did not sleep.

In the morning they let me out. The cop gave me back the watch.

I have never told anybody this.

Not my father, not my brother, not my mother, not my wife, not a sponsor, not a priest.

I am telling it now.

I thought I was going to die in there.
