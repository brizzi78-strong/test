## Concept

A picture book for ages 4–8 about what happens to love when someone we love dies —
told through the cardinal. The adult books answer *what do I do* and *why do I
stay*; this one answers the question a child actually asks: **"Where did Grandpa
go?"**

**Anchor line (already in the brand, from the memoir dedication):**
> *Those we love don't go away, they walk beside us every day.*

**Working title options:**
1. **The Cardinal's Promise: A Story for Little Ones** — maximum brand carry
2. **The Bird on the Mirror** — from the true scene; strongest standalone hook
3. **Red Feather, Big Love** — softest for the youngest readers

**Story shape (32-page standard):** A child loses a grandfather. Winter comes.
A cardinal begins appearing — on the feeder, the fence, the car mirror — at the
exact moments the child misses him most. The child learns the promise: the love
doesn't leave; it changes where it sits. Final spread: the child, older, pointing
out a cardinal to someone smaller. The promise gets passed down.

## Why this book completes the line

- **Bereavement programs need it.** Hospice bereavement coordinators, children's
  grief centers, funeral homes, and children's hospitals buy grief picture books
  in bulk and give them to families. This is a durable institutional channel the
  first two books opened.
- **The cardinal-gift market is real and evergreen** (ornaments, remembrance
  gifts). A cardinal grief book is a natural holiday/memorial gift purchase.
- **It converts the memoir's most emotional readers** into repeat, multi-copy
  buyers (one per grandchild).

## Launch window and why

**On-sale: early November 2027**, anchored to **Children's Grief Awareness Day
(third Thursday of November — Nov 18, 2027)** and running into the holiday
gifting season. Cardinals + Christmas + remembrance is the strongest seasonal
alignment in the entire campaign — handled as memorial-gentle, never exploitative.
