## Sequence blueprints

### Toolkit warm sequence (5 emails, days 0–10, to `toolkit-lead`)
1. **Day 0 — Deliver + story:** the worksheet, plus the story of the first family
   Rob sat with. One idea: you don't have to be perfect, you have to be informed.
2. **Day 2 — The costliest myth:** "qualifying isn't the same as being ready"
   (Nana's lesson). Soft mention the book exists.
3. **Day 4 — Straight answer:** Medicare vs. Medicaid in plain language. CTA: the
   full chapter is in the Toolkit.
4. **Day 7 — The objection email:** "I can just Google this." Why 25 chapters,
   worksheets, and a NC resource directory beat 40 open tabs at 2 AM.
5. **Day 10 — Close:** full offer, bulk option, one testimonial. P.S.: the memoir
   is coming January 9th — here's why that date.

### Memoir preorder sequence (4 emails, to engaged non-buyers, Oct–Dec 2026)
1. The date (17/17 — told straight, no mysticism)
2. The Cooperstown excerpt (what Dad's love looked like)
3. "You hungry?" (the Florida drive — recovery angle)
4. What preordering does for an independent author + incentive recap

### Launch week broadcast plan (Jan 5–12, 2027)

- **Memoir buyer (4 emails):** thank-you from Rob → review ask → "who did you

## Rules

1. **Segment-first sends.** Full-list broadcasts only for launches and the
   Dec 25 memorial. Everything else goes to the relevant segment(s).
2. **The Dec 25 email has no links and no ask.** Every year. Non-negotiable.
3. Every email ends with one P.S. maximum; during a launch, the P.S. is always
   the current campaign's single CTA.
4. Reader replies get personal answers. The reply rate is the platform's moat —
   protect the sender reputation and the relationship.
5. Re-engagement pass every quarter; cold subscribers (180 days, no opens)
   get one win-back, then are pruned to protect deliverability.
