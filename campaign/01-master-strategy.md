# 01 — Master Strategy

## The brand in one line

A memoir and platform built on a single promise: **show up for the people who are
dying, and for the people who love them.**

## The author

Rob Brizzi. Born Claudio in Mexico City. Adopted by Lou Brizzi in 1986. Wrestler.
Addict. Sober since January 9, 2009. Hospice liaison. Husband to Hope. Son.
Seventeen years walking into rooms most people leave.

Public credentials: **CDP** (Certified Dementia Practitioner), **Dave Ramsey Master
Financial Coach**. Nothing else. (See firewall rule, README.)

## The narrative asset: 17/17

County wrestling champion at seventeen (1995). A cardinal on the driver's-side
mirror at seventeen years sober — January 9, 2026, fifteen days after Lou died on
Christmas Day. The number is not a coincidence to be explained. It is a promise to
be kept. Every piece of campaign copy can draw on this spine; none of it should
over-explain it.

## Why three books, and why in this order

| Release | Job in the funnel | Audience it recruits |
|---------|-------------------|----------------------|
| **Toolkit** (Oct 2026) | Utility-first lead generation. Low-risk purchase, high perceived value, evergreen search demand ("Medicare vs Medicaid," "how to choose hospice"). Back matter promotes the memoir preorder. | Adult children of aging parents (45–65), caregivers, social workers, senior-living staff — NC first, national by keyword |
| **Memoir** (Jan 9, 2027) | The flagship. Converts the Toolkit list + five Kit segments into superfans; earns media (Smith Publicity); powers speaking. | Recovery community, hospice families & providers, faith readers, adoptees and adoptive parents |
| **Children's book** (Nov 2027) | Legacy + gifting title. Highest margin per unit of emotional impact; unlocks bulk buyers (hospice bereavement programs, funeral homes, children's grief centers). | Grieving families with kids 4–8; grandparents; bereavement professionals |

**Sequencing logic:** each book warms the exact audience the next one needs.
The Toolkit buyer is standing at the bed today — in January they will want the
book about the man who sits down beside them. The memoir reader who cried at the
epilogue is the first buyer of the children's book for a grandchild.

## Audience segments (Kit tag taxonomy — already live)

1. **Recovery** — the still-suffering and the long-sober
2. **Hospice & End-of-Life** — families at the bed; providers carrying the weight
3. **Faith** — undenominational, grounded, real
4. **Adoption** — the chosen and the choosing
5. **Financial Coaching** — families clawing back from the wreckage

Every campaign email, ad set, and content pillar maps to one of these five.
Each segment has its own welcome sequence; every sequence points back to the
same promise.

## Positioning statements

- **Toolkit:** *The handbook you needed the day Mom's doctor said "we should talk."*
  Not inspiration — answers. Medicare, Medicaid, dementia, assisted living, hospice,
  worksheets included. Written by someone who walks into these rooms for a living.
- **Memoir:** *This is not a book about grief. It is a book about presence — and the
  courage it takes to choose it.* Not an inspirational memoir; a working manual for
  showing up.
- **Children's book:** *Those we love don't go away — they walk beside us every day.*
  The promise, sized for the smallest hands in the room.

## Success definition

The campaign succeeds when, by December 2027:

- Kit list ≥ **10,000** engaged subscribers across the five segments
- Toolkit is a top-10 Amazon result for its core caregiving keywords
- Memoir: ≥ **5,000** copies first year, ≥ 200 Amazon ratings, 10+ earned-media hits
- Children's book: ≥ 3 institutional bulk accounts (hospice/bereavement/funeral)
- Speaking pipeline: ≥ 12 paid or strategic engagements booked from the platform

## Guardrails

1. **The firewall** (README) — no employer mentions, ever, anywhere.
2. **Steven's memory** and every named person in the memoir: placement confirmed
   with family before any excerpt is used in marketing.
3. **Grief content standard:** witness, don't perform. No manipulative countdowns
   on memorial dates. December 25 content is memorial-only, never promotional.
4. **AI disclosure** stays verbatim on Toolkit copyright page; if asked in media,
   answer plainly and move on.
5. Book content and hospice sales territory work remain fully separate.
