## Email 2 — Day 2

**Subject:** Qualifying isn't the same as being ready
**Preview:** Nana's lesson — the mistake almost every family makes once.

My grandmother qualified for a facility before she was ready to live in one.

Those are two different questions, and almost every family collapses them into
one. *Does she qualify?* is a paperwork question. *Is she ready — are we ready?*
is a life question. Answering the first one does not answer the second.

I've watched families rush a move because the paperwork said yes. I've watched
others wait a year too long because nobody would say the word "ready" out loud.

Here's the straight answer: qualification tells you what's possible. Readiness
tells you what's right. You need both, and you're allowed to work on them
separately.

This is the kind of thing I ended up writing a whole book about — 25 chapters of
the questions families actually ask me, with the worksheets to go with them.
More on that later this week.

For now, one question to sit with at dinner: *what would "ready" actually look
like — for them, and for you?*

Rob Brizzi, CDP

## Email 3 — Day 4

**Subject:** Medicare vs. Medicaid, in plain English
**Preview:** Sixty seconds, no jargon, the distinction that changes everything.

Straight answer, no jargon:

**Medicare** is health insurance for people 65 and older. It pays for hospital
stays, doctors, rehab after a hospital stay. It does **not** pay for long-term
custodial care — the years of help with bathing, dressing, and meals.

**Medicaid** is the program that pays for long-term care — but only after most
of the money is gone, and the rules about *whose* money and *when* are where
families get hurt.

The million-dollar mistake I see: families assume Medicare will cover the
nursing home. They find out it won't at the worst possible moment — in a
discharge office, with three days to decide.

The full chapter in the Toolkit walks through what each program covers, what it
doesn't, and the five-year rule that catches everyone off guard — with a
worksheet to map your parent's situation: **[LINK: Toolkit Amazon page]**

The panic isn't from the system being hard. It's from meeting it unprepared.
