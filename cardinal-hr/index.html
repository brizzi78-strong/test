The pipeline

One candidate, four stages, no dropped handoffs.

Each stage is a strict state machine — every move is allowed or it isn't, and reaching the end of one stage is what starts the next. The status words below are the real states an application, order, packet, and enrollment move through.

STAGE 01

Recruiting

Post requisitions and move applicants through the hiring pipeline.

applied→interview→offer→hired
STAGE 02

Screening

Background checks with consent captured before anything runs.

authorized→in progress→clear
STAGE 03

MyHR

New-hire paperwork, e-signed and reviewed by HR.

assigned→submitted→approved
STAGE 04

Training

Assign courses, track lessons, score the assessment.

not started→in progress→completed

What's inside

Four modules that already work — and one on the way.

Live Recruiting

Applicant tracking

Job requisitions and a hiring pipeline that won't let anyone skip a step or be quietly forgotten.

  • Requisitions: open, on hold, filled, closed
  • Enforced stages — no jumping straight to an offer
  • Reject / withdraw with a reason, on the record
  • Hiring a candidate fills the role and starts screening
Live Screening · HireCheck

Background checks

FCRA-aware screening with a pluggable data provider — Equifax wired in, others drop into the same seam.

  • No check runs before the candidate authorizes
  • Nine check types (incl. drug screening), bundled into packages
  • Adverse decisions wait out a dispute window
  • Every result kept with a full audit trail
Live MyHR

New-hire paperwork

The forms a new hire actually has to sign, tracked from assigned to approved.

  • I-9, W-4, tax, direct deposit, acknowledgments
  • Typed e-signature on the forms that need one
  • HR approves, or returns with a note to fix
  • Packet status derived from the forms themselves
Live Training

Online training portal

A customizable course catalog — Sexual Harassment Prevention, safety, code of conduct, whatever you require.

  • Courses are lessons plus an optional passing score
  • Lesson-by-lesson progress per learner
  • Assessment must pass to complete — retakes allowed
  • Completion you can prove, per employee
Next up Benefits

Choosing benefits

A guided benefits election — plan options, coverage tiers, and dependents — so a new hire picks their benefits in the same flow that hired and onboarded them. In design.

Built for the parts that get you sued

Compliance isn't a checkbox — it's the workflow.

Consent before checking

Background checks are consumer reports under the FCRA. Cardinal HR won't run one until the candidate's disclosure and authorization are on file.

A fair chance to dispute

An adverse decision issues a pre-adverse notice and holds for a waiting period, so a candidate can correct a wrong record before it costs them the job.

Everything on the record

Every state change — signed, approved, returned, cleared — is appended to an immutable history. When someone asks "what happened," there's an answer.
