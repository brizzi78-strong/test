FAQ

Questions worth answering before you buy.

Is the background-check cost included in my plan? +

No. Background checks (HireCheck) are billed per check at +$35, on top of your per-employee subscription. Screening only happens when you're actively hiring, so charging per check keeps you from paying per seat for something most of your workforce never triggers. The fee is a pass-through of the provider's report cost plus our markup.

Can I start with just hiring and add the rest later? +

Yes. Core gives you Recruiting, MyHR onboarding, Training, and the Employee Directory — enough to run hiring end to end. When you're ready for Benefits and Payroll, move up to Complete. Because it's one employee record, nothing gets re-entered when you add a module.

Is there a free trial? +

Yes — 14 days, no card required. You can run real requisitions, onboarding packets, and courses during the trial. Background checks run during a trial are billed per check at the standard +$35, since those are real provider reports.

How is "per employee" counted? +

By active employees — people currently on the directory in an employed status. Candidates and applicants in the hiring pipeline are not billed until they're hired, and offboarded employees drop off at the end of the billing period. You're billed for who actually works there, not for every record you've ever created.

Do you file my payroll taxes? +

Honestly: the Payroll module calculates gross-to-net and withholding and produces the pay run, but Cardinal HR does not itself remit or file payroll taxes with tax authorities. Production tax filing and remittance are handled through a registered payroll-tax provider connected to your account. You remain responsible for confirming filings are correct and on time — treat this as software, not a tax service.

Can I switch tiers later? +

Yes, up or down. Upgrades take effect immediately and are prorated for the rest of the billing period; downgrades take effect at the start of your next period so you keep what you've paid for. Your data and history carry across — switching tiers changes what's enabled, not what you've already recorded.
