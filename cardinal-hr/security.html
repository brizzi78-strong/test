Security & Compliance

Trust isn't a badge. It's built into the workflow.

The riskiest parts of hiring — consent, adverse action, signed forms, who-did-what — are enforced by the software itself, not left to a checklist someone forgets. Here's what's built today, how we handle your data, and what's still on the roadmap.

Cardinal HR is a working product, not a certified or independently audited service. This page describes real product behavior and our intended security posture — it does not claim any certification we don't yet hold. Where something is planned rather than built, we say so plainly.

What the code actually enforces

Compliance built into the workflow.

These aren't policies we promise to follow — they're guardrails the system won't let you skip. Each one is a real state machine: the next step can't start until the required step is on the record.

Built Screening · FCRA

Consent before any check

Background checks are consumer reports under the FCRA. Cardinal HR won't run one until the candidate's disclosure and authorization are captured — there is no path to order a report without consent on file first.

consent required→ authorized→ in progress→ clear
Built Screening · Adverse action

A waiting window before a no

When results could sink an offer, the system issues a pre-adverse-action notice and holds for a dispute window before a final adverse decision can be recorded — so a candidate can correct a wrong record before it costs them the job.

pre-adverse notice→ dispute window→ final notice
Built MyHR · Onboarding

Typed e-signatures on required forms

Onboarding forms that need a signature — I-9, W-4, direct deposit, acknowledgments — capture a typed e-signature and move from assigned to submitted to approved. A packet's status is derived from the forms themselves, not set by hand.

assigned→ e-signed→ submitted→ approved
Built Every module

An append-only audit trail

Every state change across Recruiting, Screening, MyHR, and Training — signed, approved, returned, cleared, completed — is appended to an immutable history. Entries are added, never edited or deleted. When someone asks "what happened, and when," there's an answer.

event→ appended→ immutable

Principles, stated honestly

How your data is handled.

These are the principles the product is designed around. Some are enforced in code today; the infrastructure statements below describe our intended posture, not an audited guarantee.

One record, one truth

A candidate becomes an employee on a single record that every module reads and writes. Nothing is re-keyed between hiring and onboarding, so there's one place a person's data lives — and one place to correct or remove it.

Least data

We collect what a hiring and HR workflow actually needs to function — no speculative data hoarding. Sensitive fields, like screening results, are tied to the workflow step that requires them.

Role-based access

Access is scoped by role as a design principle — a hiring manager, an HR reviewer, and a new hire don't see the same things. Granular, configurable role controls are an area we're continuing to build out.

On encryption and hosting, stated as intended posture: data is intended to be encrypted in transit and at rest, and the product is intended to run on reputable cloud infrastructure. We are not publishing specific ciphers, key-management details, or uptime figures, and none of this should be read as a certified or contractually guaranteed control. If your procurement needs those specifics in writing, talk to us and we'll tell you honestly where things stand.

FAQ

The questions security teams actually ask.

Are you SOC 2 certified? +

Not yet — and we'd rather say so than imply otherwise. Cardinal HR does not currently hold a SOC 2 report, and we have not undergone a formal third-party security audit. A SOC 2 Type II is on our roadmap.

What we can point to today is product behavior: enforced consent, adverse-action waiting periods, e-signed forms, and an append-only audit trail. If your organization requires a completed SOC 2 before adopting a vendor, we're not there yet — ask us where the audit stands before you plan around it.

Where is my data hosted? +

The product is intended to run on reputable cloud infrastructure, with data intended to be encrypted in transit and at rest. We're describing intended posture here, not a certified or contractually guaranteed configuration, and we're not publishing a specific region, provider, or key-management scheme on this page. For a written answer tied to your procurement or data-residency requirements, contact us directly.

Do you handle FCRA compliance for me? +

Partly, and it's important to be precise. The platform enforces the mechanics: it won't run a background check without the candidate's consent on file, and it holds an adverse decision through a pre-adverse-action notice and dispute window. Those steps are built in and can't be skipped.

But the employer remains the party responsible for FCRA compliance. Cardinal HR is software that helps you follow the process correctly — it is not your compliance department and not legal advice. You're responsible for confirming your obligations, and your provider agreement, with qualified counsel.

Do you file my payroll taxes? +

No. The Payroll module computes gross-to-net and withholding, but Cardinal HR does not itself remit or file payroll taxes with tax authorities. Production filing and remittance are handled through a registered payroll-tax provider connected to your account. Treat this as software, not a tax service — you remain responsible for confirming filings are correct and on time.

Can I export my data? +

Because everything lives on a single employee record, your data is yours to take with you — records and their history can be exported. Richer, per-field audit exports are on the roadmap; if you have a specific export format or a data-portability requirement, tell us what you need and we'll tell you honestly what's available today versus what's planned.
