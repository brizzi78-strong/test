# The Little Lantern Fox

*A bedtime picture book for ages 3–7*

---

**Title page**

# The Little Lantern Fox

*Story for small people who are not quite sleepy yet.*

> **Illustration:** A tiny orange fox sits on a hilltop at dusk, holding a
> paper lantern on a stick. The lantern is dark. Stars are just beginning
> to appear.

---

**Dedication**

*For everyone who has ever been a little afraid of the dark —
and gone out anyway.*

---

## Spread 1

When the sun went down behind the blueberry hills,
every animal in Willow Hollow lit a light.

The fireflies lit their bellies.
The lighthouse lit its eye.
The moon lit the whole wide sky.

> **Illustration:** Wide view of Willow Hollow at twilight — cozy burrows
> and nests glowing warmly, fireflies rising like sparks, a lighthouse on
> the far shore, a big friendly moon.

---

## Spread 2

Everyone had a light.

Everyone except Pip.

Pip was a very small fox
with a very small lantern
that would not light at all.

> **Illustration:** Pip shaking his paper lantern upside down, one ear
> flopped over, frowning at it. A snail watches sympathetically.

---

## Spread 3

“Lanterns don’t light for foxes,” said the owl,
who knew almost everything.

“Then how will I find my way in the dark?” asked Pip.

“Ah,” said the owl. “That is the part I don’t know.”

> **Illustration:** A huge, kind owl peering down from a branch, moon
> behind her like a halo. Pip looks very small below.

---

## Spread 4

So Pip set off into the dark anyway,
holding his unlit lantern high,
because holding it high felt braver
than holding it low.

> **Illustration:** Pip marching into tall dark grass, lantern raised like
> a flag. Only his ears and the lantern show above the grass.

---

## Spread 5

In the dark he heard a *snuffle*.

A hedgehog was crying.
She had dropped all her blackberries
and could not find a single one.

“I can’t see them,” she sniffed.

“Neither can I,” said Pip. “Let’s not see them *together*.”

> **Illustration:** Pip and the hedgehog on hands and knees, bottoms up,
> feeling around in the dark. Blackberries scattered everywhere, gleaming
> faintly.

---

## Spread 6

They found the blackberries one by one,
by touch, and smell, and giggling.

And when they were done —
*flick* —
one tiny flame woke up inside Pip’s lantern.

> **Illustration:** Close-up of the lantern with a single small flame,
> reflected in both animals’ wide eyes.

---

## Spread 7

In the dark he heard a *whimper*.

A duckling had waddled the wrong way home
and the pond was nowhere at all.

“Hold my tail,” said Pip. “We’ll be lost together,
and that’s only half as lost.”

> **Illustration:** A little parade: Pip in front with the lantern (one
> flame), duckling holding his tail, hedgehog holding the duckling’s wing.

---

## Spread 8

They followed the frog songs all the way to the water,
and the duckling’s mother honked so happily
the reeds shivered.

*Flick. Flick.*

Two more flames woke up inside Pip’s lantern.

> **Illustration:** Joyful reunion at the pond, moonlight on the water,
> the lantern now glowing noticeably brighter.

---

## Spread 9

All night long, Pip walked through the dark
and the dark was full of friends who needed one.

A beetle stuck on his back. *Flick.*
A mouse afraid of the owl. *Flick.*
The owl, embarrassed, afraid of the mouse. *Flick.*

> **Illustration:** Three small vignettes in a row — Pip flipping the
> beetle, Pip introducing mouse and owl (both hiding behind their wings
> and paws), lantern brighter in each one.

---

## Spread 10

By the time Pip reached the top of the blueberry hills,
his little lantern blazed like a bonfire —

so bright that the fireflies came to warm their toes,
so bright the lighthouse blinked twice, jealous.

> **Illustration:** Pip on the hilltop, lantern radiant, animals gathered
> around in its glow, fireflies orbiting like tiny planets.

---

## Spread 11

“Lanterns don’t light for foxes,” hooted the owl. “How?”

Pip looked at the hedgehog, the duckling,
the beetle, and the mouse.

“It doesn’t light for me,” he said.
“It lights for *us*.”

> **Illustration:** All the animals leaning together in a warm circle of
> light, shadows soft behind them.

---

## Spread 12

And that is why, in Willow Hollow,
if you are ever lost in the dark,
you don’t look for the moon,
and you don’t look for the lighthouse.

You look for a very small fox —

> **Illustration:** View from far away in the dark woods: one warm light
> bobbing between the trees.

---

## Spread 13

— and you won’t be lost anymore.

**The End.**

> **Illustration:** Pip asleep in his burrow, lantern hanging by the door,
> still softly glowing. Through the round doorway, all of Willow Hollow
> twinkles.

---

## Back cover

*Pip’s lantern won’t light — no matter how hard he shakes it.
But somewhere out in the dark, a hedgehog has lost her blackberries,
a duckling has lost his pond, and everyone has lost a little bit of brave.
Maybe lanterns aren’t lit the way anyone thinks they are…*
