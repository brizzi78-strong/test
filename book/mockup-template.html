Dedication
For Lou.

You gave me your name —
and a lifetime of showing me how to wear it.
✿
2

It started with a ballpark, big as the whole sky.

Grandpa held my hand and said,
“Watch close, kiddo. The best players aren’t the loudest ones. They’re the ones who show up — every single game.”

3

After that, we didn’t need a stadium.

The backyard was enough — one ball, two gloves, and a hundred summer evenings of catch.

“Being there,” Grandpa said, “is most of what love is.”

4

You gave me your name. Brizzi. I was just a little boy when I got it, and it fit like a cap two sizes too big.

“Don’t worry,” you smiled. “It’s a name to grow into.”

I never stopped trying to grow into it.

I didn’t always get it right.

Some days the ball missed my glove, and the promise felt too big for me — like everything else I was growing into.

But Grandpa stayed right there.

He didn’t fix it. He didn’t rush it. He just stayed —

and bumped his fist against mine, soft as a promise.

Some promises don’t need words.

Some are made in a hug that says:
I’m here. I’ll stay. I’ve got you.

Author’s Note
Lou gave me more than his name. He gave me a way of being in the world: show up, stay close, keep your word. I learned it at Yankee Stadium and in the backyard, one game of catch at a time.

Today I carry that promise into hospice rooms, where showing up is the whole job and staying is the whole gift. This little book is my way of saying the promise was received — and it is still being kept.

— Rob Brizzi

The Wedding

I wore a tiny tux the day I learned what a promise sounds like out loud.

“You say it once,” Grandpa told me, “and then you spend your whole life keeping it.”

Look — a Cardinal

“Look,” Mom whispered. “A cardinal.”

We always knew who it was. Some people show up so often, you learn to look for them everywhere.

Any Game Counts

Some days it was soccer instead of baseball.

The game never mattered. The showing up did.
