# Me and Dad: The Way It Should Have Been

**By Rob Brizzi** · Brizzi House Publishing · 2026
*Companion to "The Promise: A Name to Grow Into" and "The Cardinal's Promise"*

**Core theme:** An empty page isn't the end of a story — it's an invitation
to write. The should-have-beens become instructions for the dad you choose
to become.

**Alternate titles:** *The Book of Should-Have-Beens* · *All the Games We
Never Played* · *The Days We Should Have Had*

**Tone:** Gentle, honoring, never bitter. The first half lives inside the
imagined childhood (warm golden light); the turn acknowledges the truth
(cooler, quieter light, the cardinal arriving); the ending is warm again —
the promise carried forward. Same color-temperature symbolism as the
swimming spread: cool for the hard truth, warm for safety and hope.

## Page 9 — Author's Note

> This is the book I couldn't read as a boy, because nobody wrote it.
> My dad and I didn't get the Saturday mornings, the sidelines, the
> long games of catch. For a long time that felt like an ending.
>
> It was Lou who showed me the other way to read an empty page — as a
> place where you get to write. Show up. Stay. Keep your word. I write
> those words now with my own hands, in my own family, and at bedsides
> where showing up is the whole gift.
>
> If your book has empty pages too, this one is for you.
> Pick up the pen.
>
> — Rob Brizzi
