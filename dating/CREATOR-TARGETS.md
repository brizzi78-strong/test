# Finding your first 10 campus creators (UNC · Duke · NC State · Wake Forest)

A repeatable way to source, vet, and land ~10 women micro-creators — roughly
2–3 per school — to be **Founding Creators** for Cardinal. This is the worksheet;
you fill the names in. (No fabricated names or follower counts here — pull those
live and verify them, because they change weekly.)

Target profile: **women, 5k–50k followers, real engagement, campus-relevant
content, brand-safe, and a vibe that fits "done with drunk frat guys / safety
first."** Micro > mega here — a real 12k-follower junior out-converts a rented
500k account.

---

## 1. How to find them (do this per school)

**Instagram**
- Search the campus location tag and school hashtags: `#UNC #Tarheels`,
  `#DukeUniversity #Duke27`, `#NCState #Wolfpack`, `#WakeForest #WFU`, plus
  `#[school]sorority`, `#[school]greeklife`, class-year tags (`#unc2027`).
- Open the tagged photos at campus spots (Franklin St, Bryan Center, the
  Brickyard, Reynolda) and see who posts a lot and gets real comments.
- Check sorority chapter accounts → their social chairs and most-tagged members
  are often creators.

**TikTok**
- Search `[school] sorority`, `[school] grwm`, `[school] day in my life`,
  `[school] dating`, `get ready with me [school]`. The algorithm will surface
  the campus creators fast once you engage.
- Look at who's making "college dating / talking stage / situationship" content
  — that's your exact message-fit.

**Shortcuts**
- Ask your **first campus ambassadors** the magic question: *"Who does everyone
  at your house/dorm actually follow?"* Humans beat search here.
- Creator-discovery tools (e.g., an influencer-search platform) can filter by
  location + follower band + engagement if you want to scale the search.

## 2. The vetting scorecard (score each candidate 1–5)

| Criterion | What "5" looks like |
|---|---|
| **Fit to message** | Posts dating/campus/lifestyle; would credibly say "I'm done with the frat scene" |
| **Engagement rate** | Comments/likes are real and high for their size (aim >3–5%), not a bought/ghost audience |
| **Audience = ICP** | Followers are actually women at/near that school |
| **Follower band** | 5k–50k (micro). Don't over-index on raw count |
| **Brand-safe** | No content that clashes with a safety-first women-first brand |
| **Reliability** | Posts consistently; responds; would actually show up |

Green-light candidates that score 4–5 on **fit, engagement, and audience** — those
three matter more than follower count.

**Red flags:** sudden follower spikes (bought), comments that are all emoji/bots,
engagement way below size, or content at odds with the safety brand.
