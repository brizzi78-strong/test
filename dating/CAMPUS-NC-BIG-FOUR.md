# Attracting the NC "Big Four" — UNC, Duke, NC State & Wake Forest

A concrete campus-launch plan for North Carolina's four ACC schools — the
"Big Four" of Tobacco Road:

- **UNC-Chapel Hill** (~31k students) — Chapel Hill
- **Duke** (~17k) — Durham
- **NC State** (~37k, the largest) — Raleigh
- **Wake Forest** (~9k, small private) — Winston-Salem

The first three form a dense **Triangle cluster** (all within ~30 minutes of
each other) — that's your launch core. **Wake Forest is ~1.5 hours west** in
Winston-Salem, so treat it as a **fast-follow satellite**, not part of the
same-night cross-campus events. All four share the Tobacco Road rivalry, which
is the story that ties the whole thing together.

The motion is the same women-first sequence everywhere: **seed the women, use
the rivalry and real events as the hook, gate with `.edu` verification for
trust and exclusivity.**

---

## 1. The wedge: exclusivity + safety + rivalry

- **.edu-gated launch.** Early access requires a verified `unc.edu`,
  `duke.edu`, `ncsu.edu`, or `wfu.edu` email. Exclusive (students want in),
  safe (real, verified students only), and a clean self-contained network per
  school to reach liquidity fast.
- **Safety is the switch.** Students — and their parents — care about safety
  more than any group. "Every member is background-checked and verified, and
  it's only real Big Four students" lands where "meaningful connections" never
  will.
- **The rivalry is the story.** "Date across Tobacco Road." Cross-school is a
  feature, not a footnote — fun, shareable, and press-worthy.

---

## 2. Two clusters, one playbook

- **Triangle core — launch together:** UNC + Duke + NC State. They're close
  enough to share cross-campus events and a single dense liquidity pool. This
  is where you prove the model.
- **Wake Forest — fast follow:** launch it as its own local network once the
  Triangle is humming. Its small, tight-knit campus means you can hit density
  *fast* with fewer people. It joins the big rivalry mixers as **destination
  events** (a Triangle trip), not weeknight meetups.

---

## 3. Seed the women first (the whole game)

Recruit a few hundred women **per campus, by hand**, before opening it up:

- **Greek life & women's orgs.** Panhellenic sororities, women-in-STEM/business
  groups, a cappella, club sports, grad cohorts. Wake Forest and Duke have
  especially strong, well-networked Greek scenes — one warm intro cascades.
- **Paid campus ambassadors.** 4–8 well-connected women per school who run
  events and bring their networks. Highest-ROI launch spend, bar none.
- **Grad & professional schools** (older, higher intent, more able to pay for
  Cardinal+ later): Kenan-Flagler (UNC MBA), Fuqua (Duke MBA), Poole (NC State),
  Wake Forest School of Business, plus law/med/vet/design.
- **The ask is personal** — founder/ambassador DMs and coffee chats, then
  "invite three friends." Not ads.

Only after the women's side is dense do you open the men's side — and let the
"the women are already here" waitlist sell it.

> **Ratio note for NC State:** it's the biggest and skews STEM/engineering, so
> it has a large male population. That's *good* for the paying men's side later
> — but it makes seeding women first even more important there. Guard the ratio
> hardest at State.

---

## 4. Events = the acquisition engine

Real events make the safety promise tangible and create word of mouth no ad can
buy. Run them near campus:

- **Cross-Triangle "date across Tobacco Road" mixer** — the flagship, rotating
  between Chapel Hill, Durham, and Raleigh venues.
- **Game-watch parties** for ACC basketball — the biggest social moments of the
  year here. Build the calendar around them; peak on the rivalry games.
- **Coffee-shop speed dating:** Franklin Street (UNC), Ninth St / Brightleaf
  (Duke), Hillsborough Street (NC State), downtown Winston-Salem / Reynolda
  Village (Wake).
- **Low-stakes daytime events** (run club, trivia, a cappella night) so it's not
  only nightlife — matches the safe, low-pressure brand.
- **Wake Forest:** its own local mixers + hyped bus trips to the big Triangle
  rivalry events.

Capacity + waitlist creates demand; "free for women, priority for Cardinal+"
ties events to the model.

---

## 5. On-the-ground channels (by campus)

- **Tabling & flyering** at the highest-traffic spots: the Pit / Franklin St
  (UNC), Bryan Center Plaza (Duke), the Brickyard / Hillsborough St (NC State),
  Manchester Plaza / the Mag Quad (Wake). QR → `.edu` signup.
- **Dorm/house leaders & RAs** as micro-ambassadors.
- **Campus TikTok/IG** with school-specific, rivalry-flavored humor and real
  member stories — students share what's *theirs*.
- **Student papers & radio:** The Daily Tar Heel (UNC), The Chronicle (Duke),
  Technician / WKNC (NC State), Old Gold & Black (Wake). The safety +
  women-first + rivalry angle is a real story, not an ad.
- **Local partnerships:** coffee shops and bars for venues and member perks
  (a free coffee for verified members is cheap and sticky).

---

## 6. Timing

- **Launch at the start of fall semester** ("syllabus week") — social demand
  peaks and calendars are open.
- **Ride basketball season** (Nov–Mar) with game-watch events; peak around the
  rivalry games.
- **Avoid finals and summer** for the hard push.
- Stagger: prove the **Triangle** in the fall, bring **Wake Forest** on in the
  spring (or the next fall) once the playbook is repeatable.

---

## 7. Referral & exclusivity mechanics

- **Waitlist by school**, with a visible counter ("312 women at UNC already
  in"). Scarcity + proof.
- **Invite-a-friend for status, not discounts:** move up the waitlist, get event
  priority. Aspirational and on-brand.
- **House / dorm / org leaderboards** and **school-vs-school** signup
  competitions — leans straight into the rivalry.

---

## 8. What to measure (per campus)

- Verified women signed up (leading indicator) and **men:women ratio** — guard
  it; if it tips male (watch NC State), pause male signups.
- Time-to-first-match for a new woman.
- Event attendance and repeat rate.
- Invites sent per active woman (is the flywheel spinning?).

---

## 9. Do-not

- Don't open all four to everyone on day one — seed women first per campus, or
  the ratio collapses and the network dies.
- Don't treat Wake Forest as a fourth Triangle school — the drive breaks
  weeknight cross-campus events; give it its own local motion.
- Don't buy ads before liquidity — waste on an empty network.
- Don't over-promise safety you can't operationally deliver — on a campus, one
  bad incident travels instantly. Background checks and verification must be
  real, not marketing.

---

*Bottom line: launch `.edu`-gated across the Triangle three (UNC, Duke, NC
State) together, hand-recruit a few hundred women per campus through Greek life,
clubs, and paid ambassadors, and make rivalry-themed "date across Tobacco Road"
events the thing everyone hears about. Bring Wake Forest on as a fast-follow
once the playbook repeats. Exclusivity gets them in the door; safety and real
connection keep them.*
