# Cardinal — naming: keep it, or change it?

The name is worth a real look before launch. "Cardinal" has genuine upside and
some genuine baggage. This is the case for each, a set of alternatives, and a
recommendation. **No verdict is locked** — if you pick a direction, the demo can
be renamed end to end.

---

## The case *for* keeping "Cardinal"

- **The symbolism is perfect.** Cardinals mate for life and stay through every
  winter — that's the entire brand thesis in one bird. Hard to beat.
- **It's the NC state bird** — on-brand for a Carolina launch.
- **It's already built** — logo, red palette, the whole demo, the pitch.
- Warm, memorable, easy to say and spell.

## The case *against* (why you raised it)

- **Sports-team collision — and it's an ACC one.** The **Louisville Cardinals**
  are an ACC school. For an app built on the NC/Tobacco Road ACC rivalry, naming
  it after a rival's mascot is awkward at best. Add **Stanford Cardinal**, the
  **MLB St. Louis Cardinals**, and **NFL Arizona Cardinals** — the word is
  crowded and sports-coded.
- **Internal brand clash.** This repo already has **"Cardinals Promise" (CARD
  token)** and a caregiver app under the Cardinal name. A dating app sharing the
  name muddies all three.
- **Trademark & domain risk.** "Cardinal" is heavily trademarked across
  industries; a clean app-store name + `.com` + USPTO mark will be hard to
  secure. (Needs a real trademark/domain search — see caveat below.)
- **Undifferentiated** in a category where a distinct name travels.

---

## What a good name needs to do here

1. **Ownable** — available `.com` (or a strong alt), app-store name, and a
   clearable trademark in dating/software.
2. **No team/mascot collision**, especially not an ACC one.
3. **Evokes the thesis** — safety, staying, real/lasting connection — or at
   least doesn't fight it.
4. **Short, sayable, spellable**; works as a verb/handle ("meet on ___").
5. **Not male-coded** (kills anything like "Wingman") — this is women-first.

---

## Alternatives, by direction

**A. Staying / loyalty / mate-for-life** (keeps the thesis, drops the mascot)
- **Nest** — home, building something, warmth. Strong; check trademark (common).
- **Roost** — "come home to roost," cozy, ownable-sounding.
- **Perch** — where birds pair up; short, brandable, verb-friendly.
- **Evergreen** — stays green all winter; loyalty without a bird.
- **Steadfast / Stay** — on-thesis but generic; harder to own.

**B. Other mate-for-life symbols** (love without the Cardinal baggage)
- **Dove** — mates for life, universal love symbol, warm. (Common word — check
  marks.)
- **Wren / Lark / Finch** — small songbirds; softer, less sports-coded than
  Cardinal. (We already use "Wren" as a demo profile — reusable as a brand.)

**C. North Carolina, without a mascot**
- **Dogwood** — the **NC state flower**; warm, distinctive, gender-neutral,
  local, and *not* a team. Strong dark-horse.
- **Longleaf** — the NC state tree (longleaf pine); rooted, evergreen, ownable.

**D. Warmth / staying through winter**
- **Hearth** — home, warmth, gathering; safe and intimate. Very on-brand.
- **Ember** / **Kindling** — a spark that lasts; a bit used in dating already.

---

## Recommendation

If the sports/ACC collision bothers you (it should, for this launch), the two
strongest pivots are:

1. **Dogwood** — keeps the Carolina identity and the warmth, ditches the mascot
   problem entirely, and is distinctive and likely more ownable.
2. **Hearth** or **Nest** — leans all the way into the safety/home/"stay"
   thesis, nationally scalable beyond NC.

If you'd rather **keep Cardinal**, that's defensible — the symbolism is genuinely
the best of any option — but then plan around the collisions: secure the app
name early, differentiate hard from the teams, and separate it cleanly from the
existing "Cardinals Promise" brand.

---

## Important caveat

I have **not** verified domain or trademark availability for any name here — a
real launch needs a proper `.com`/app-store/USPTO search (and ideally a
trademark attorney). Treat the above as creative direction, not clearance.

and (b) draft a shortlist to run through domain + trademark checks.
