# Our Why — Cardinal's founder story

*A short, honest origin story for the About page, the pitch, press, and creator
outreach. No competitor is named — it's about the experience, not a platform.
Edit the voice until it sounds like you; the [brackets] are yours to fill.*

---

## Draft (first person)

In 2017, I met my wife online.

It changed my life. But somewhere in the middle of it — the messaging, the
meeting up, the quiet hope that the person on the other end was really who they
said — I kept coming back to the same thought: there's almost nothing here
actually looking out for us. No real way to know someone was who they claimed.
No one checking. If something went wrong, you were on your own.

When we matched, her profile said — in all caps — **"READ MY PROFILE FIRST."**
She was that tired of it: the lazy "hey," the guys who clearly hadn't read a
single word, the noise that made her feel like a swipe instead of a person. What
she wanted wasn't complicated — **character and substance.** A real person, not a
highlight reel. That line is half the reason I paid attention. It's also half the
reason Cardinal exists.

So we did it the old-fashioned way: before we ever met in person, we talked. Our
first date was a phone call. You can hear character and substance in someone's
voice in a way a hundred texts never show you — and it's a lot harder to hide
who you are. It's part of why Cardinal nudges your first date toward a call:
old school, safer, and real.

I got lucky. I found the person I'd build my life with.

But *lucky* isn't good enough — not when what's on the line is whether someone
feels respected, and whether they get home safe.

Cardinal is the app I wish had existed back then, for everyone still out there
looking for what I found. Every member is verified and background-checked before
they can reach you. Every match opens on something real — so no one gets a "hey"
into the void, and no one has to beg you to read their profile. You decide who
gets to talk to you. You can share your date with someone you trust and check in
with one tap. We built it women-first, because women carry the most risk — and
deserve the most protection.

Cardinals mate for life. This is for anyone hoping to.

— [Your name], founder

## Notes for editing

- **Keep it generic about the "other app."** "online" / "a dating app" / "the
  apps that exist now" — never a brand name. (You said it: no free plugs.)
- **Optional to add, if you want it more personal:** a single concrete moment
  that stuck with you — a scary near-miss, a friend's bad experience, the first
  time you thought "this isn't safe enough." One real detail makes the whole
  thing land. Drop it in after "you were on your own."
- **The tone to aim for:** reassuring and protective, not salesy. You're not
  selling an app; you're telling people you see the risk they're taking and
  you built something to carry it with them.
- **Where it goes:** About page, the top of the pitch, your first press
  interview, and the opener when you recruit campus creators ("here's why I'm
  building this").
