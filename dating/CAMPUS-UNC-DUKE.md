# Attracting UNC & Duke students to Cardinal

A concrete campus-launch plan for the two anchor schools of the Triangle
beachhead: **UNC-Chapel Hill** and **Duke (Durham)** — ~10 miles apart, dense,
social, and joined by the most famous rivalry in college sports. That rivalry
is a gift: use it.

The motion is the same women-first sequence as the main GTM plan, executed on
two campuses at once. **Seed the women, use the rivalry and events as the hook,
gate with .edu verification for trust and exclusivity.**

---

## 1. The wedge: exclusivity + safety + rivalry

- **.edu-gated launch.** Early access requires a verified `unc.edu` /
  `duke.edu` email. This does three things at once: makes it *exclusive*
  (students want in), makes it *safe* (real, verified students only), and gives
  you a clean, self-contained network to reach liquidity fast.
- **Safety is the switch.** Students — and their parents — care about safety
  more than any group. "Every member is background-checked and verified, and
  it's only real UNC and Duke students" is a message that lands on campus in a
  way "meaningful connections" never will.
- **The rivalry is the story.** "Date across the line." Cross-campus is a
  feature, not a footnote — inherently fun, inherently shareable, inherently
  press-worthy.

---

## 2. Seed the women first (the whole game)

Recruit ~200–300 women per campus **by hand** before opening it up:

- **Greek life & women's orgs.** Panhellenic sororities, women-in-STEM/business
  groups, a cappella, club sports, grad-program cohorts. One warm intro per org
  cascades.
- **Campus ambassadors — paid.** Hire 4–8 well-connected women per school as
  paid reps who run events and bring their networks. This is the single highest-
  ROI spend at launch.
- **Grad & professional schools.** Fuqua (Duke MBA) and Kenan-Flagler (UNC MBA),
  law, med — slightly older, higher intent, and (relevant to monetization) more
  able to pay for Cardinal+ on the men's side later.
- **The ask is personal.** Founder- and ambassador-led DMs and coffee chats, not
  ads. Then: "invite three friends."

Only after the women's side is dense do you open the men's side — and let the
"the women are already here" waitlist do the selling.

---

## 3. Events = the acquisition engine (campus edition)

Real events are where the safety promise becomes tangible and where word of
mouth is born. Run them near campus:

- **Cross-campus mixer** at a Durham/Chapel Hill venue — the flagship "date
  across the rivalry" night.
- **Game-watch parties** for UNC–Duke basketball — the single biggest social
  moment of the year in this market. Build the calendar around it.
- **Coffee-shop speed dating** on Franklin Street (UNC) and Ninth Street /
  Brightleaf (Durham).
- **Low-stakes daytime events** (run club, trivia, a cappella night) so it's not
  only nightlife — matches the safety, low-pressure brand.

Every event → photos, stories, and new signups. Capacity + waitlist creates
demand; "free for women, priority for Cardinal+" ties it to the model.

---

## 4. On-the-ground channels

- **Tabling & flyering** at the highest-traffic spots: the Pit and Franklin
  Street (UNC), Bryan Center Plaza and the BC Walkway (Duke). QR → .edu signup.
- **Dorm & house leaders / RAs** as micro-ambassadors.
- **Campus TikTok/IG** with school-specific, rivalry-flavored humor and real
  member stories — not generic app ads. Students share what's *theirs*.
- **Student newspapers & radio:** The Daily Tar Heel, The Chronicle — the
  safety + women-first + rivalry angle is a genuine story, not an ad.
- **Local partnerships:** coffee shops and bars for event venues and
  member perks (a free coffee for verified members is cheap and sticky).

---

## 5. Timing

- **Launch at the start of fall semester** ("syllabus week") — social demand
  peaks, everyone's meeting new people, and the calendar is open.
- **Ride basketball season** (Nov–Mar) with game-watch events; peak around the
  UNC–Duke games.
- **Avoid finals and summer** for the hard push (campus empties out).

---

## 6. Referral & exclusivity mechanics

- **Waitlist by school** with a visible counter ("312 women at UNC already in").
  Scarcity + proof.
- **Invite-a-friend for status, not discounts:** move up the waitlist, get event
  priority. Keeps it aspirational and on-brand.
- **House / dorm / org leaderboards:** a little inter-group competition drives
  signups and plays into the rivalry theme.

---

## 7. What to measure (per campus)

- Verified women signed up (the leading indicator) and **men:women ratio** —
  guard it; if it tips male, pause male signups.
- Time-to-first-match for a new woman.
- Event attendance and repeat rate.
- Invites sent per active woman (is the flywheel spinning?).

---

## 8. Do-not

- Don't open both campuses to everyone on day one — seed women first, or the
  ratio collapses and the network dies.
- Don't buy ads before liquidity — waste of money on an empty network.
- Don't over-promise safety you can't operationally deliver — on a campus, one
  bad incident travels instantly and ends the trust. The background check and
  verification must be real, not marketing.

---

*Bottom line: launch `.edu`-gated at UNC and Duke, hand-recruit a few hundred
women per campus through Greek life, clubs, and paid ambassadors, then make the
rivalry-themed cross-campus events the thing everyone hears about. Exclusivity
gets them in the door; safety and real connection keep them.*
