# Getting elite Instagram & TikTok creators to promote Cardinal

At the four NC campuses (UNC, Duke, NC State, Wake Forest), the fastest way to
reach women is through the **women they already follow** — campus micro-creators
on Instagram and TikTok. This is the acquisition channel that fits a women-first,
invite-only, safety brand. Here's how to land them.

The ICP to remember: **sorority women who are tired of drunk frat guys.** The
creators you want are *those women, with an audience.* Their followers are the
exact people you're trying to admit.

---

## 1. Who to recruit (not mega-influencers)

- **Campus micro-creators: 5k–50k followers.** Higher engagement, more trust,
  far cheaper than big names, and their audience is literally their classmates.
  A 15k-follower UNC junior converts better here than a 500k national creator.
- **Prioritize women** (the side you're seeding) who post lifestyle, "get ready
  with me," dating/relationship takes, campus life, sorority content, fitness,
  or "day in my life."
- **Sorority social chairs & Panhellenic leaders** — often already creators, and
  they carry a whole house's network.
- **A few well-chosen guys** later (for the men's side), but women first.

**How to find them:** search school hashtags/locations (#UNC, #DukeUniversity,
#NCState, #WakeForest, campus geotags), TikTok "[school] dating/sorority,"
and ask your first ambassadors "who does everyone at your house follow?"

---

## 2. The offer (why they say yes)

Creators promote what makes *them* look good to their audience. Lead with
status and mission, not just cash:

- **Founding Creator status** — a title, an exclusive invite-code to give their
  followers, early access, and profile in the app as a founding member.
- **Their own vanity invite code + waitlist link** (e.g., `MADDIE-UNC`) so their
  audience skips the line — and you can **track** exactly who drove signups.
- **Comp structure:** a flat launch fee ($100–$500 for micro-creators) **plus**
  performance (per verified-woman signup, or per referral). Perf-based keeps it
  honest and scales with results.
- **Free Cardinal+ / event perks** (host a mixer, bring friends).
- **The mission angle:** "help make dating safer for women on your campus."
  For this ICP, that's genuinely motivating and very postable.

---

## Lead ad copy (the hook that speaks to the ICP)

> **"Are you tired of drunk college guys? The first dating app that puts your
> safety first."**

That's the flagship campus headline — it names the exact feeling of the target
(sorority women over the frat-party scene) and answers it with the core promise
(safety first). Use it as the hook for paid social, flyers, and creator posts.

Supporting taglines:
- *"The first real dating app that looks out for you."*
- *"The others don't care about you. Cardinal does."*
- *"Invite-only. Background-checked. No drunk DMs."*
- *"Cardinals mate for life. Find yours."*

**Competitive contrast angle.** The incumbents (Tinder, Hinge, Bumble) optimize
for *your engagement* — time in app, swipes, subscriptions — not *your safety or
your outcome*. "The others don't care about you" names that plainly: they profit
when you keep swiping; Cardinal wins when you actually find someone and leave.
Use it as the wedge in creator posts and paid social — but keep it truthful and
punchy, not a smear (don't name competitors in a way that invites legal trouble;
"the others" does the work).

## 3. What they post (content that actually converts)

Authentic > polished ad. Give a loose brief, not a script:

- **The relatable hook:** "POV: you're done with drunk frat-party 'talking
  stages'" → "so I joined the invite-only one that background-checks everyone."
  That's the ICP's exact feeling; it does numbers.
- **The exclusivity flex:** "I got into the invite-only dating app — here's my
  code for the first 50 girls." Scarcity + a gift to followers.
- **The safety explainer:** verified, background-checked, interview to get in.
  Reassuring to women *and* their parents.
- **Event content:** film the mixers/speed-dating — the best organic ad there
  is, and it proves the app is real and alive.
- **Get-ready-for-a-Cardinal-date** style vlogs once there are real dates.

Always end with **their code + waitlist link** in bio/caption.

---

## 4. The mechanics that make it work

- **Unique code per creator** → attribution. You know who drives verified
  women, and you double down on them.
- **Waitlist link** (the demo already has the waitlist) so a spike in demand
  doesn't wreck the ratio — you let women in first, on pace.
- **Exclusive codes create FOMO:** "first 50 girls" caps supply and drives
  urgency. The invite-only model *is* the marketing.
- **Ambassadors ↔ creators overlap:** your paid campus ambassadors (from the
  campus plan) are often your best creators. Same people, two hats.

---

## 5. Rollout sequence per campus

1. **Recruit 3–5 women creators** per school before launch (start with the ones
   your ambassadors already know).
2. **Seed them first** — get them in the app, verified, loving it, *before* they
   post. Authenticity requires they actually use it.
3. **Coordinate a launch burst** — several creators post in the same week so it
   feels like "everyone at [school] is talking about Cardinal." Density of
   voices, same as density of users.
4. **Tie posts to the first event** — "join + come to the mixer Thursday."
5. **Feed the winners** — whoever drives the most verified women gets more
   budget, better titles, event-hosting.

---

## 6. Budget reality (order of magnitude)

- Micro-creator launch fees: **$100–$500 each**; 4–5 per school × 4 schools =
  a few thousand dollars for a whole-Triangle creator launch.
- Performance bonuses scale with signups — the good kind of cost.
- Far cheaper per verified woman than paid social ads on an empty network, and
  it builds real word of mouth instead of renting attention.

---

## 7. Don'ts

- **Don't chase follower counts** over engagement/fit — a bought 200k audience
  converts worse than a real 10k campus one.
- **Don't hand them a stiff script** — canned "#ad" posts flop; let them sound
  like themselves.
- **Don't let creator hype outrun the ratio** — route demand through the
  waitlist so the men's side doesn't flood in ahead of the women.
- **Don't skip disclosure** — paid posts need #ad/#sponsored (FTC). It also
  reads as more trustworthy, not less, to this audience.

---

*Bottom line: recruit ~4–5 women micro-creators per campus, make them Founding
Creators with their own invite codes, let them post the real "done with drunk
frat guys → joined the safe invite-only one" story, and route every follower
through the waitlist so women get in first. Their audience is your ICP; their
codes are your attribution.*
