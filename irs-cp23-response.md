To Whom It May Concern:

I am writing in response to Notice CP23 for tax year 2025, dated June 8, 2026,
which changed my return and assessed additional tax of $7,500 by disallowing the
New Clean Vehicle Credit (Internal Revenue Code § 30D) I claimed on Form 8936.
**I respectfully disagree with this change and request that the credit be
reinstated.** The notice states the credit was disallowed because the vehicle
identification number (VIN) reported on my return did not match IRS records.
That is a reporting/matching discrepancy — not a question of my eligibility — and
I meet every requirement for the credit.

**1. I am within the income limitation.** I filed Married Filing Jointly. My
modified adjusted gross income is below $250,000, well under the $300,000 MFJ
threshold under § 30D, and it was under the threshold in both 2025 and the
preceding year. Income is not a basis for disallowance.

**2. The vehicle qualifies.** I purchased a new 2025 Tesla Model Y Long Range
All-Wheel Drive (Order No. RN125485754, ordered August 12, 2025; delivered
[DELIVERY DATE]). As a sport utility vehicle, its MSRP limit is $80,000; the
vehicle's price (base $48,990) is far below that cap. It is a qualifying
manufacturer's clean vehicle and was purchased for personal use, not resale.

**3. The VIN discrepancy originates with the seller's report, not my
eligibility.** Under § 30D, the seller (Tesla, Inc.) was required to file a
time-of-sale report through the IRS Energy Credits Online (ECO) portal
identifying my VIN. The "VIN does not match our records" determination indicates
that the seller's time-of-sale report for my vehicle is missing, was rejected,
or was filed with an incorrect VIN or other incorrect data. I have requested the
seller's time-of-sale report and a corrected filing from Tesla and am working to
resolve the discrepancy at the source.

**4. Requested relief.** I ask that the IRS (a) reinstate the $7,500 New Clean
Vehicle Credit; or (b) if the discrepancy cannot yet be resolved, identify the
specific VIN on record so I can reconcile it against my purchase documents and
the seller's filing. I am submitting this disagreement within the timeframe
stated in the notice and I expressly preserve my right to appeal and my right to
petition the U.S. Tax Court.

Enclosed are copies supporting my position. Please contact me at the phone number
or address above if you need anything further.
