# Interview Questions — Expansion Zones

Ghostwriter questions to draw out material for the four open expansion zones
(zone 3, year-one sobriety, is written; zone 4, the ch-1 tease, was defused).
Answer in any order, any length; dictated fragments are fine.

## Zone 1 — The hospice rooms

1. Walk me into one specific room you've never forgotten. What was on the nightstand?
2. What's the first thing you do with your hands when you walk into a family's room? Where do you stand or sit, and why there?
3. Tell me about a family that got it wrong at first — arguing, avoiding, phones out — and what turned the room.
4. What's something a dying person said to you that you've never told anyone?
5. Has a patient ever reminded you so much of Lou that you had to step out? What happened in the hallway?
6. What do people always ask you at a bedside that you can't answer? What do you say instead?
7. Tell me about a death that went well. What did the family do right in the last hour?
8. What's the strangest or funniest thing that's ever happened in a hospice room?
9. What did Dr. Huet see you do that made him say what he said?
10. Is there a smell, sound, or piece of equipment that says "hospice" to you the way the wrestling room had its smell?

## Zone 2 — Lou before you

1. What do you know about Lou's childhood — his parents, where he grew up, what his father was like to him?
2. He had three kids by twenty-two. What did he tell you about being that young, that broke, that overwhelmed?
3. What was his work, exactly, in his prime? A day in his working life — what time he left, what he carried, who he answered to.
4. What's the story of his first marriage ending, as much as you know and are willing to print?
5. Did Lou ever tell you about a failure that embarrassed him?
6. Where did the temper come from? What did it cost him with Lisa, Joel, and Jon?
7. What did Lou do when he was alone? The thing he did on a Sunday with nobody watching.
8. How did he decide, at forty-something, to take on a widowed immigrant's two boys? Did he — or Lisa or Lynda — ever say why?
9. What's one story about Lou that his first three kids tell that you weren't there for?
10. What was Lou like as a young man in love — courting, nervous, human?

## Zone 5 — Hope's side

1. What does Hope say the first year of dating you was actually like — her version?
2. What did her friends and Rhonda warn her about when they heard "in recovery"?
3. What does she do on January 9 every year?
4. What's the one habit from your addict years that survived into sobriety and drives her crazy?
5. During the breakdown year after Lou died — what did she later say those nights felt like from her side of the bed?
6. What's her own loss story — what did she carry into the marriage that you had to learn to hold?
7. When has she been afraid you'd drink? Has she ever said it out loud?
8. What's a moment where she showed you what staying looks like — her version of the U-Haul?
9. What would she say the book gets wrong or leaves out about her? (Ask her. Print the answer.)
10. Would she write one page in her own voice that could run in the book?

## Zone 6 — Mexico at 26

1. What was the trip supposed to be for — and what was it actually for?
2. Were you high, drunk, or white-knuckling it when you saw Horacio?
3. What did Horacio look like in the first three seconds?
4. What language did you speak with him, and what got lost?
5. What did he show you or give you? A photo, a room, a meal, an object?
6. Was there a moment he saw through you — or a moment you lied to his face?
7. Did you go anywhere from your childhood? What was smaller than you remembered?
8. What did you feel on the flight home, and what did you tell people the trip had been?
9. Who else did you meet who knew you as Claudio? What did they call you?
10. What was the 26-year-old actually looking for down there, and why couldn't he find it drunk?
