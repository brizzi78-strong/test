New York ★

The New York edition of It’s Not Your Fault — every office, program, and form, named.
This companion names the New York version of every office the main book points to, plus a few benefits unique to the Empire State. Numbers were current at press time — confirm before relying on them. The state your parent lives in is what governs.

Who to reach in New York

What you needWho to reach
Medicaid & long-term careNew York Medicaid — apply through your Local Department of Social Services (LDSS); in NYC, through HRA. Long-term care is delivered through Managed Long Term Care (MLTC); enroll via NY Medicaid Choice, 1-888-401-6582.
Free Medicare counseling (SHIP)HIICAP — Health Insurance Information, Counseling & Assistance Program — 1-800-701-0501.
Prescription help (NY-specific)EPIC (Elderly Pharmaceutical Insurance Coverage), for residents 65+ — 1-800-332-3742. Works alongside Medicare Part D.
Report abuse, neglect, exploitationAdult Protective Services — through your county Department of Social Services (NYC: 212-630-1853). When unsure, NY Connects (1-800-342-9871) will route you.
Aging services / one-stopNY Connects — 1-800-342-9871 (aging.ny.gov). NYC Aging: 212-244-6469.
Portable medical order (Ch. 23)New York uses the MOLST form (Medical Orders for Life-Sustaining Treatment) — ask the doctor.
Advance directive (Ch. 20–21)New York Health Care Proxy (names a health care agent), often paired with a Living Will.
Veterans (Ch. 18)NYS Department of Veterans’ Services (veterans.ny.gov).
New York extras families miss.
  • Enhanced STAR — a larger school-property-tax exemption for homeowners 65+.
  • HEAP (Home Energy Assistance Program) — help with heating/cooling bills.
  • EISEP (Expanded In-home Services for the Elderly) — in-home help for those not on Medicaid; ask your county Office for the Aging.
  • EPIC (above) can lower drug costs even for higher incomes than families expect.

How to use this with the book

Keep this page with your copy of It’s Not Your Fault. Where a chapter says “call your state Medicaid office” or “ask which portable-order form your state uses,” this is the New York answer. Start with NY Connects (1-800-342-9871) when you’re not sure who to call — its whole job is routing New York families to the right door.
