New Jersey ★

The New Jersey edition of It’s Not Your Fault — every office, program, and form, named.
This companion names the New Jersey version of every office the main book points to, plus a few benefits unique to the Garden State. Numbers were current at press time — confirm before relying on them. The state your parent lives in is what governs.

Who to reach in New Jersey

What you needWho to reach
Medicaid & long-term careNJ FamilyCare (New Jersey Medicaid) — apply at 1-800-701-0710 or your County Board of Social Services. Long-term care is delivered through MLTSS (Managed Long Term Services & Supports) within NJ FamilyCare.
Free Medicare counseling (SHIP)New Jersey SHIP (State Health Insurance Assistance Program) — 1-800-792-8820 (Division of Aging Services) to reach your county SHIP office.
Prescription help (NJ-specific)PAAD (Pharmaceutical Assistance to the Aged & Disabled) and Senior Gold — 1-800-792-9745. State drug help beyond Medicare Part D.
Report abuse, neglect, exploitationAdult Protective Services 24-hour hotline 1-800-792-8820 (or your county APS; state office 609-588-6501).
Aging services / one-stopAging & Disability Resource Connection (ADRC) — 1-877-222-3737 (nj.gov/humanservices/doas). Also NJ 2-1-1 for local help.
Portable medical order (Ch. 23)New Jersey uses the POLST form (Practitioner Orders for Life-Sustaining Treatment) — ask the doctor.
Advance directive (Ch. 20–21)New Jersey Advance Directive for Health Care: a Proxy Directive (naming a health care representative) plus an Instruction Directive (living will).
Veterans (Ch. 18)NJ Dept. of Military & Veterans Affairs (nj.gov/military); Veterans Benefits Hotline 1-888-865-8387.
New Jersey extras families miss. Apply for several at once through NJ Save (njsave.org):
  • Senior Freeze (Property Tax Reimbursement) & ANCHOR — property-tax relief for older homeowners; 1-800-882-6597.
  • Lifeline utility assistance — help with electric/gas bills.
  • JACC (Jersey Assistance for Community Caregiving) — in-home help for seniors not yet on Medicaid; ask the ADRC.
  • Office of the Ombudsman for the Institutionalized Elderly — for problems in a facility; 1-877-582-6995.

How to use this with the book

Keep this page with your copy of It’s Not Your Fault. Where a chapter says “call your state Medicaid office” or “ask which portable-order form your state uses,” this is the New Jersey answer. Start with the ADRC (1-877-222-3737) when you’re not sure who to call — its whole job is routing New Jersey families to the right door.
