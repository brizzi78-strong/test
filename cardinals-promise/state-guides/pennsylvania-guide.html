Pennsylvania ★

The Pennsylvania edition of It’s Not Your Fault — every office, program, and form, named.
This companion names the Pennsylvania version of every office the main book points to, plus a few benefits unique to the Keystone State. Numbers were current at press time — confirm before relying on them. The state your parent lives in is what governs.

Who to reach in Pennsylvania

What you needWho to reach
Medicaid & long-term carePennsylvania Medical Assistance (Medicaid) — apply at COMPASS (compass.state.pa.us) or your County Assistance Office. Long-term care is delivered through Community HealthChoices (CHC); enroll via PA Enrollment Services, 1-844-824-3655.
Free Medicare counseling (SHIP)PA MEDI (Pennsylvania Medicare Education & Decision Insight; formerly APPRISE) — 1-800-783-7067.
Prescription help (PA-specific)PACE / PACENET (Pharmaceutical Assistance Contract for the Elderly), 65+ — 1-800-225-7223.
Report abuse, neglect, exploitationOlder Adults Protective Services statewide elder-abuse hotline — 1-800-490-8505 (24/7).
Aging services / one-stopPA Link to Aging & Disability Resources — 1-800-753-8827; or your local Area Agency on Aging (pa.gov/aging).
Portable medical order (Ch. 23)Pennsylvania uses the POLST form (Pennsylvania Orders for Life-Sustaining Treatment) — ask the doctor.
Advance directive (Ch. 20–21)Pennsylvania Advance Health Care Directive: a Living Will plus a Health Care Power of Attorney.
Veterans (Ch. 18)PA Dept. of Military & Veterans Affairs (dmva.pa.gov); 1-800-547-2838.
Pennsylvania extras families miss.
  • Property Tax / Rent Rebate Program — for older homeowners and renters; 1-888-222-9190.
  • LIHEAP — help with home heating bills.
  • OPTIONS — in-home services (personal care, meals) for older adults not yet on Medicaid; ask your Area Agency on Aging.
  • PACE / PACENET (above) covers many seniors who assume they earn too much.

How to use this with the book

Keep this page with your copy of It’s Not Your Fault. Where a chapter says “call your state Medicaid office” or “ask which portable-order form your state uses,” this is the Pennsylvania answer. Start with your Area Agency on Aging or PA Link (1-800-753-8827) when you’re not sure who to call — their whole job is routing Pennsylvania families to the right door.
