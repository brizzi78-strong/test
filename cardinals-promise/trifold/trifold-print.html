Do not wait and see

When to Act Now

  • Falls — especially a second one
  • Wandering
  • Unsafe driving
  • Medication errors
  • Scams that cost real money
  • No food / spoiled food
  • Stove left on
  • Sudden confusion
Sudden confusion is often reversible — infection, dehydration, or medications. Get a medical evaluation before assuming the worst.
In every appointment

Key Questions to Ask

  • Is this reversible?
  • Is rehab needed?
  • Is home still safe?
  • What does Medicare cover?
  • Do we need Medicaid planning?
    See an elder-law attorney before moving any money.
  • Is hospice appropriate?
    Any family member can call for a free evaluation.

Hour by hour

First 24 Hours

  • Safety first
  • Get the medication list
  • Find insurance cards
  • Ask hospital status: inpatient or observation?
  • Identify decision-maker / POA
  • Write down what changed — with dates
"Observation" days may not count toward the 3-day inpatient stay Medicare requires before covering rehab. Ask early — and ask in writing.
Building the plan

Next 48–72 Hours

  • Call the doctor — ask "Is this reversible?"
  • Call siblings — short, factual update
  • Area Agency on Aging — Eldercare Locator 1‑800‑677‑1116
  • SHIP free Medicare counseling — 1‑877‑839‑2675 · shiphelp.org
  • Social services office for Medicaid — Medicaid.gov
  • Start the emergency binder
Dementia crisis, any hour: Alzheimer's Association 24/7 Helpline 1‑800‑272‑3900. Local help anywhere in the U.S.: dial 211.
