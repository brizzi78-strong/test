# Aging Parent Cardinal's Toolkit — Trifold Crisis Companion

A laminated, fridge-worthy quick-reference companion to the full handbook
(*Aging Parent Cardinal's Toolkit*, Cardinal's Promise Series). It is the
bird's-eye map; the full toolkit is the deep woods guide.

**Format:** US Letter (11" × 8.5"), landscape, trifold (roll fold), printed
both sides, laminated.

## Panel map

A trifold has six physical panels. The seven content blocks map like this:

| Sheet | Position | Content |
|---|---|---|
| Outside | Left (fold-in flap) | When to Act Now |
| Outside | Center (visible back when folded) | Key Questions to Ask + contact / QR / tagline footer |
| Outside | Right | Front cover |
| Inside | Left (inside flap) | Start Here: What Happened? |
| Inside | Center | First 24 Hours |
| Inside | Right | Next 48–72 Hours |

The "back middle" (Key Questions) and "back panel" (website / contact / QR)
share the center outside panel: questions on top, contact block below. That
panel is the visible back of the folded brochure, so the tagline and QR code
land exactly where a reader flips to.

## Inside center — First 24 Hours

- ☐ Safety first
- ☐ Get medication list
- ☐ Find insurance cards
- ☐ Ask hospital status: **inpatient or observation** (it changes what Medicare pays)
- ☐ Identify decision-maker / POA
- ☐ Write down what changed — with dates

---

## Inside right — Next 48–72 Hours

- ☐ Call doctor — ask: *"Is this reversible?"*
- ☐ Call siblings — short, factual update
- ☐ Contact Area Agency on Aging — Eldercare Locator **1-800-677-1116**
- ☐ Call NC SHIIP for Medicare questions — **ncdoi.gov/shiip**
- ☐ Call county DSS for Medicaid / Special Assistance questions — **epass.nc.gov**
- ☐ Start the emergency binder

*Dementia crisis at any hour: Alzheimer's Association 24/7 Helpline
**1-800-272-3900**. Local help anywhere in NC: dial **211**.*

---

## Back left (fold-in flap, outside) — When to Act Now

These are not "wait and see." Act today:

- Falls — especially a second one
- Wandering
- Unsafe driving
- Medication errors
- Scams that cost real money
- No food / spoiled food
- Stove left on
- Sudden confusion — often reversible (UTI, dehydration, medications): get an evaluation

---

## Back middle — Key Questions to Ask

- Is this reversible?
- Is rehab needed?
- Is home still safe?
- What does Medicare cover?
- Do we need Medicaid planning? *(See an NC elder-law attorney before moving any money.)*
- Is hospice appropriate? *(Any family member can call for a free evaluation.)*
