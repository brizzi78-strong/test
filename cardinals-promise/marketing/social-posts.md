## 5. Burnout / permission to rest

**Short:**
> You cannot save someone by destroying yourself.
>
> A caregiver who collapses doesn't produce more care — they produce a second
> emergency on top of the first. Resting isn't quitting. Asking for help isn't
> failing. 🐦 *It's Not Your Fault.*

**Long (great for LinkedIn):**
> Here's the line I most want every family caregiver to read:
>
> **You cannot save someone by destroying yourself.**
>
> Somewhere in a long caregiving stretch, a quiet belief takes hold — *if I
> just try harder, sleep less, and carry more, I can hold it all together
> myself.* It feels like love. It feels like loyalty. But run it to the end and
> it removes the one person your parent depends on most.
>
> The strongest thing you can do for the person you're caring for is to still
> be standing next week, and the long stretch neither of you can see the end of
> yet. Protecting yourself *is* protecting them.
>
> That's a whole chapter in *It's Not Your Fault.* Out now. [BUY LINK]
