# Beta Reader Kit — The Caregiver's Cardinal Toolkit

> Purpose: get 5–10 real readers through the book before KDP upload, and
> settle the open question Rob assigned to readers: do the five part-divider
> art pages stay? Feedback collects itself in the Jotform below.

## The feedback form (live)

**https://form.jotform.com/261936560720054**

Eleven questions, ~5 minutes: wayfinding (1–5), the divider-art vote
(keep / professional artwork / remove), the 30-second boxes, the five
pathways, the RED/PURPLE/BLUE system, most-helpful chapter, anything
confusing or wrong, and a 0–10 recommend score.

## Who to ask (aim for 5–10, in this priority)

1. **Hope** — she's also verifying Chapters 17, 25, 2, 3, and 27–28.
2. **Two or three current caregivers** — people mid-journey right now;
   they test the 11pm-hallway usefulness better than anyone.
3. **One colleague from home health or hospice** — a professional eye on
   whether anything reads wrong at the bedside.
4. **One person who has never faced this** — if they can navigate it cold,
   anyone can.
5. **One recent widow/widower or adult child post-loss** — the last-days
   chapters deserve one reader who has lived them. Ask gently; make it
   easy to say no.

## Invitation email (copy-paste)

Subject: Would you read my book before it goes to print? (2–3 hours, honest opinions wanted)

Hi [Name],

I've finished my book — The Caregiver's Cardinal Toolkit, a color-coded
workbook for families caring for an aging parent — and before it goes to
the printer I need a few people I trust to read it the way a real family
would.

You don't need to read every page. Skim it the way you'd actually use it:
find the part that matches (or once matched) your family, read that
section properly, and flip through the rest. Then tell me the truth on
this short form — it takes about five minutes:

https://form.jotform.com/261936560720054

One specific thing I need your vote on: the five full-page cardinal
drawings that open each part of the book. Keep, replace, or cut — the
readers decide, not me.

If you can get it back to me by [date — 2 weeks out], you'll be in the
acknowledgments, and a finished copy is yours when it prints.

Thank you — this means more than you know.

Rob
[phone] · [email]

## Logistics

- Attach the current PDF (`book/cardinals-toolkit-book.pdf`, 222 pages)
  or share it however each reader prefers; printed spiral copies from a
  local print shop are worth it for the least tech-comfortable readers.
- Give a **2-week deadline** — open-ended beta reads never come back.
- **Do not rebut feedback.** Thank every reader, note everything, act on
  patterns (two or more readers saying the same thing = real).
- Decision rules, set in advance:
  - **Dividers:** majority "keep" → they stay; majority "professional
    artwork" → commission the cover designer for the same five slots;
    majority "remove" → delete the five `.divider` blocks + trailing
    blank and rebuild (page count and spine revert).
  - **Anything flagged confusing by 2+ readers** gets rewritten, not
    defended.
  - **Recommend score:** average 8+ = ship; below 8 = one more revision
    pass on the weakest chapters first.
- After the round: results feed the final proof pass in
  `publishing/kdp-checklist.md`, and beta readers who agreed go in the
  acknowledgments.
