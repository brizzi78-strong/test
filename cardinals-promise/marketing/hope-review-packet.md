# For Hope — What the Book Needs From You

If you're credited in the book (as Project Consultant, or "with Hope Brizzi,
PharmD"), here is everything that needs your words or your sign-off. Total
writing: roughly 250 of your own words. Total time: about an hour, most of it
reading.

## 1. The two "Pharmacist's Note" boxes — rewrite or approve (~120 words each)

These exist as drafts written *for* you. If your name goes on them, they should
be yours. Rewrite freely, or edit the drafts, or approve as-is.

**Chapter 17 (Prescription Assistance) — draft:**
> Two things I wish every family knew. First, your pharmacist can see what your
> doctor often cannot: every prescription, from every prescriber, on one screen
> — and we are the easiest person in healthcare to reach. No appointment
> needed; just ask for a minute at the counter. Second, never let the price at
> the register be the end of the conversation. Ask us to check the cash price,
> a different pharmacy, a 90-day fill, and whether the prescriber should hear
> about an alternative. The counter price is a starting point, not a verdict.

*Prompt if rewriting: What do families get wrong at the pharmacy counter?
What would you tell them to ask you?*

**Chapter 25 (Medication and Doctor Lists) — draft:**
> When a family hands me a current, complete medication list, the whole visit
> changes — we catch duplicates, interactions, and doses that no longer fit
> the person. When they hand me a bag of bottles, I am guessing about what is
> actually being taken. Bring the list. Update it every time something
> changes. And use one pharmacy if you can — it is the only way any pharmacist
> ever sees the whole picture.

*Prompt if rewriting: What changes for you when a family is organized? What's
the one habit you wish every caregiver had?*
