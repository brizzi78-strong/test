# Cover Vote — Reader Panel Decision (July 2026)

Rob put three cover candidates to the same five-reader panel that beta-read
the book ("let them decide"). Candidates, shown as faithful layout mockups:

- **A — White minimal:** cream field, gold line art, navy serif (the
  *Being Mortal* register; previously the approved direction)
- **B — Navy premium:** navy field, gold/cream type, utility row, color tabs
- **C — Sunset question:** retitles the book "What Do I Do With My Aging
  Parent?", warm sunset photo, "North Carolina" scope line

## The vote

| Reader | Vote | Core reason |
|---|---|---|
| Denise (mid-crisis caregiver) | C | "My own sentence looking up at me" — it answers her midnight search |
| Tom (cold reader) | C | Only cover that answers the question in his head; A "looks like a book about grief" |
| Marta (hospice RN) | B | Only cover that says *workbook, tools, do-this-next*; sunset+cardinal = condolence card |
| Grace (widow) | A | A reads chaplain-gentle; "a cardinal against a sunset says the visit is ending" |
| Kevin (engineer) | B | Wins the 100px thumbnail on contrast math; icon row justifies the price |

**Result: 2 (C) / 2 (B) / 1 (A) — B wins the tiebreak:**
1. Thumbnail test went to B, 3–2 (even Grace conceded B pops when small).
2. C's design is disqualified under the panel's own two-reader rule: both
   clinical/lived-experience readers (Marta, Grace) independently identified
   cardinal-at-sunset as memorial/condolence imagery that would frighten
   buyers whose parent is alive.
3. Both C voters flagged the "North Carolina" cover line as a dealbreaker
   for non-NC buyers — it narrows a national book on its face.

**The panel's synthesis (Marta):** "B's design, C's question as marketing
copy, lose the sunset."

## What was adopted

- **Navy is the cover** — `publishing/kdp-cover-wrap.html` rebuilt in the
  navy language with the required fixes: real ISBN (978-1-963342-03-1; the
  -02-7 on the AI mock is checksum-invalid), CDP credential, concrete
  utility labels, Ferrera endorsement leading the back, color-tab strip,
  author-photo slot. **No kicker** (Rob removed "WORKBOOK").
- **Series-wide:** *A Cardinal's Promise* will also be navy (Rob's call) —
  palette locked in the designer brief (navy #1b2440, gold #c9a45c,
  cream #f5f1e8).
- **C's question survives as marketing copy:** "What do I do with my aging
  parent?" leads the Amazon A+ banner, ad headlines, and website hero
  (added to `marketing/amazon-listing.md`) — off the cover, where its
  design failed the sympathy-register test.
- **Thumbnail verified:** 100px/60px render test passed — title legible,
  navy pops on Amazon white, utility row degrades cleanly.
