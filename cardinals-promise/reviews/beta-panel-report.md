## Verdicts

- **Divider art: KEEP AS-IS, 5–0.** Unanimous — every reader independently
  said the plain, almost-homemade quality is the point ("looks like something
  a grieving son drew at his kitchen table, not something a publisher
  bought"). Decision rule satisfied: dividers stay; no commissioned art.
- **Recommend average: 8.8** (9/9/9/8/9) — above the 8.0 ship gate.
- **Wayfinding: 4/5 across the board.** Everyone found their chapter in
  under five minutes; three readers noted the front matter stacks several
  routing devices (acknowledged, deliberately not restructured — it works).
- **30-second boxes: 5/5 from all five readers.** "Those boxes ARE the
  book." (Tom)
- **Clinical accuracy (Marta):** "Nothing in the clinical content rang
  false at the bedside… which almost never happens in family-facing books."

## Fixes applied (all in this commit; $217 verified against CMS CY2026)

**Two-reader patterns (mandatory under the rules):**
1. Start Here table row rerouted: "coming home from the hospital" now →
   Chapter 6 (Home Health), with Chapter 10 noted if rehab comes first
   (Denise + Tom).
2. The Medicare SNF money clock now lives where readers land: Ch 10's
   30-second box and Ch 13 both state days 1–20 / days 21–100 at **$217/day
   (2026)** / nothing after 100, with "confirm at medicare.gov"; Pathway 1
   gained the same figure (Denise + Kevin).
3. Choose Your Path tree rebuilt from 8 to 9 questions (Tom + Marta):
   - NEW Q3: "Has a doctor mentioned hospice, comfort care, or that time
     may be short?" → Chapters 11–12 and Part 5 (the tree previously never
     routed anyone to the book's best material).
   - Q6 now catches the gradual-decline reader: "Yes, but something is
     slowly slipping → Chapter 1: start the change log" (Tom's exact gap).
   - Answer order made Yes-first consistently; "you are the smart one who
     came early" softened to "you have the gift of time."

**High-value single-reader fixes (applied by judgment):**
4. Ch 29's bedside line contradicted the Author's Note — rewritten to the
   truth: "I was not with my father when he died… it is the reason this
   book exists. If you are given the hour I missed…" (Grace — integrity
   catch of the round).
5. QIO fast-appeal added to the Numbers That Matter tear-out, and Ch 10
   now says where to find your QIO if the notice is lost —
   qioprogram.org / 1-800-MEDICARE (Denise).
6. Ch 27 "Who do we call first?" checklist now opens with: on hospice with
   comfort-focused goals, hospice is usually the first call (Marta).
7. Hospice bereavement support duration (~13 months) added to Ch 12, twice
   (Marta).
8. NEW "The Weeks After" passage closes Ch 29: bereavement care, grief
   groups, "none of it means you are going crazy — it means you loved
   someone" (Grace + Marta).
9. Future Planners list fixed: Ch 26 no longer mislabeled blue (it is red —
   explained as "it wears red because of when you will need it"); Long-Term
   Care Insurance and VA Benefits added to the list (Kevin's contradiction
   catch).
10. "Just before Chapter 1" (wrong by 19 pages) corrected in both places to
    "right after the Contents" (Tom).
11. Ch 11 now names home-based palliative care specifically (Marta).
12. Ch 27 "What to say" list: checkboxes removed beside the sacred words —
    the list stays, the little squares go (Grace: "ticking a box beside 'I
    forgive you' feels like inventorying the sacred").
13. How to Use gains the spouse/partner paragraph: "wherever it says
    'parent,' read 'the person you love'" (Grace).
14. Ch 16's 30-second box gains one buy-side line for the plan-ahead reader
    (typical buying window, premiums rise with age, fee-only advisor)
    (Kevin).
