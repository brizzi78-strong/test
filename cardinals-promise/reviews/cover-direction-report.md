# Cover Direction Report — What the Audience Wants

Two independent studies, one question: what cover converts this book's buyer?

## Study 1 — Buyer panel (three personas, four candidate covers)
- **Crisis Daughter** (buys most, on her phone at 11pm): ranked the utility-
  forward design first; "the icon row is the whole sale." Flagged "HERO
  GUIDE" as exhausting: "I don't feel like a hero. I feel like I'm failing."
- **Planner Son** (the validator): wants spec-sheet structure and the CDP
  credential — "the professional cover omitting the qualification is
  backwards." "Guidance You Can Trust" is an unverifiable claim next to
  three verifiable ones.
- **Gift Buyer**: wants warmth she can wrap; the cartoon toolbox would be
  "the slap"; navy read as "the facility's billing office."
- **Consensus:** utility structure wins the sale; warmth wins the gift;
  the winning cover needs both.

## Study 2 — Category market research (verified sources)
The category's three giants — The 36-Hour Day, How to Care for Aging
Parents, Being Mortal — share one formula: **typography-first, warm cream
ground, ONE saturated accent color.** Cardinal red on cream is exactly that
formula. Key rules: the title owns 40-60% of cover height and is the only
thing that must read at 100px; the subtitle is the sales copy (coverage
promise); credentials and series strips sell trust; sentimental stock
scenes and clutter are the classic indie mistakes; style the cardinal as a
herald, never a mourner (the sympathy-gift market owns the mournful
cardinal, and this buyer's parent is alive).

## The decision (author-approved)
The white/minimal cardinal design is the main cover direction — it passes
the 100px acid test (red bird, big title, cream ground) and matches the
category formula — with the panel's four additions: full title THE
CAREGIVER'S CARDINAL TOOLKIT, the CDP credential under the author name,
a concrete utility row (no "Guidance You Can Trust"), and the corrected
subtitle. Full designer brief: publishing/back-cover-copy.md.

Retired for this title: the navy design (kept as brief history), the bench
painting (gift-book aesthetic), the cartoon toolbox (undermines authority).
The cream dogwood design remains the NC companion workbook's cover.
