The idea

Everything hangs on one image and one sentence.

The image: a red bird that landed and stayed. The sentence: show up for the people who are dying, and for the people who love them.

The two books are the same promise through two doors. The Cardinal's Promise is the why — the story, the father, the seventeen years. The Aging Parent Cardinal's Toolkit is the what — the manual for the family standing at the bed. Every branding decision below exists to make that pairing legible at a glance: same red, same bird, same voice — different ground.

The marks

Two marks, one bird. Simple enough to draw on a napkin, which is the test.

The Perch — press mark
A red bird, landed on a line. The line is a branch, a mirror, a bed rail. Use on spines, title pages, the site header, email footers.
The Toolbox — utility mark
Marks every worksheet and action page inside the Toolkit and its lead magnets. Never appears on the memoir.

Rule: the Perch belongs to the press and both books. The Toolbox belongs to the practical line only. Neither mark ever gets a gradient, an outline, or a drop shadow.

Typography

A book voice and a clipboard voice.

Display & body — Spectral (print) / Charter (web fallback)

He kept finding me.

Serif carries every sentence a reader feels: titles, chapter openers, body text, pull quotes. Short sentences deserve a face with weight.

Utility — system sans, uppercase, letterspaced

Voice

The brand sounds like Rob writes.

You don't have to do this perfectly. You have to do the next thing. The register: grounded, direct, short sentences. Witness, don't perform.

Always

  • Short sentences. Plain words. "Medicare vs. Medicaid" not "navigating coverage complexities."
  • Name people: Lou, Hope, Jaye, Tyler. The dying person is never abstracted.
  • Let the numbers be facts, not mysticism: 17 years, 15 days, one bird.
  • One idea per piece; one CTA per email.

Never

  • No performed grief: no stock-photo tears, no violin copy, no countdown timers on memorial dates.
  • No employer mentions, anywhere, ever (the firewall).
  • No "journey," "battle," or "inspiring" as crutch words.
  • Dec 25 is memorial-only. Every year. No links, no ask.
