# The Cardinals Strategy — Promise + Toolkit as One System

How the two existing works fit together, per the [thesis](BOOK_SALES_THESIS.md) and
[roadmap](MY_BOOK_ROADMAP.md):

- **The Cardinals Promise** — the raw memoir of caring for Dad. The heart. Sells the
  feeling: *you are not alone in this.*
- **The Cardinals Toolkit** — the practical guide for caregivers. The help. Sells the
  answer: *here's how to survive it.*

One book is a story, the other is a service — and each one is the marketing engine
for the other. This is [plan §9](BOOK_MARKETING_PLAN.md) ("the best marketing for a
book is the next book") already built. Most memoirists have to invent a second
product; you have it.

## Why this niche actually works

Caregiving is one of the rare memoir niches that behaves like practical nonfiction:

- The audience is **enormous and self-identifying** — tens of millions of family
  caregivers in the US alone, most thrown into it unprepared, actively searching for
  both emotional companionship and practical help.
- It is **findable**: caregiver podcasts, grief podcasts, Facebook caregiver-support
  groups, subreddits (r/CaregiverSupport, r/AgingParents, r/dementia), hospice and
  disease-specific organizations, AARP-adjacent media. The podcast ladder from the
  roadmap has a ready-made slate.
- The **cardinal already means something** to this exact audience: the widely shared
  folk belief that a cardinal's visit is a loved one saying *I'm still here*. Grieving
  caregivers know this symbol on sight. The brand does emotional work before a single
  word is read — cover, colors, imagery, and hashtags all inherit it for free.

## The funnel — how the two works feed each other

```
Short-form story content (scenes from the Promise)
        │
        ▼
Free reader magnet: "The Cardinal's Quick Toolkit"
(a 5–10 page distillation of the Toolkit — the checklist a new caregiver
 needs in week one) → email signup
        │
        ▼
LAUNCH 1: The Cardinals Promise (memoir, $0.99 week → $4.99)
   back matter → Toolkit preview chapter + magnet link
        │
        ▼
LAUNCH 2 (4–8 weeks later): The Cardinals Toolkit (full, $7.99–$9.99)
   back matter → the Promise + magnet link
        │
        ▼
Every future reader of either book enters the same loop
```

Decisions this locks in:

| Decision | Call | Rationale |
|---|---|---|
| What's the reader magnet | A **short excerpt of the Toolkit** (quick-start checklist), not a memoir chapter | A drowning caregiver grabs a lifeline faster than a story; the memoir's job is done by the content that *led* them there |
| Which launches first | **The Promise** (memoir) | The story is what podcasts book you for and what short-form content is made of; the Toolkit converts the audience the Promise gathers |
| Gap between launches | **4–8 weeks** | Close enough that memoir momentum carries; far enough for two distinct launch weeks and two promo stacks |
| Toolkit price | Higher than the memoir ($7.99–$9.99 vs $4.99) | Practical nonfiction carries premium pricing; it's bought as a tool, not entertainment |
| Series branding | Shared "Cardinals" cover system (same cardinal motif, same typography, different dominant color per book) | Instant visual read-through on the Amazon also-boughts and the author page |

## The three hook framings to test (roadmap Phase 1, now concrete)

Publish scenes from the Promise using all three framings, ~2 pieces/week, and let
response data pick the packaging for both books:

1. **The Promise framing (love/duty):** "Before my Dad `<died / couldn't speak for
   himself>`, I made him a promise. This is what keeping it cost — and what it gave
   me." → If this wins, the memoir leads the brand and the title stays *The Cardinals
   Promise* front and center.
2. **The Caregiver framing (the unspoken reality):** "Nobody hands you a manual when
   you become your father's caregiver. Here's what I wish someone had told me." →
   If this wins, the Toolkit moves forward in every pitch and the memoir is sold as
   "the story behind the guide."
3. **The Cardinal framing (grief/sign):** "Every time a cardinal lands outside my
   window, I keep a promise to my Dad." → If this wins, the symbol leads: content is
   built around cardinal moments, and the grief/afterloss audience (adjacent to
   caregivers, even larger) becomes the primary target.

All three are true; the audience decides which is *magnetic*. That's gate G2.

## Adapted pitch slate (gate G3)

Work the podcast ladder within these lanes, smallest first:

- **Caregiver shows** — family-caregiver, dementia/Alzheimer's, cancer-caregiver,
  hospice and end-of-life podcasts (the Toolkit is the hook here)
- **Grief and loss shows** — grieving a parent, signs-and-continuing-bonds themes
  (the Promise and the cardinal are the hook here)
- **Story shows** — memoir/personal-narrative podcasts (the raw story is the hook)

Pitch angle by lane, one-sheet per lane, same book. Also uniquely available in this
niche and not in most: **support organizations** — caregiver nonprofits, hospice
bereavement programs, and support-group leaders will share a resource that helps
their people. Pitch them the *free Quick Toolkit*, not the books; the funnel does
the rest. Organizations won't sell for you, but they will *help* for you.

## What stays the same

Everything else runs exactly per the [roadmap](MY_BOOK_ROADMAP.md): the 6-month gated
timeline, the 5–10 hr/week schedule, one content platform + podcast ladder, no ads,
no launch date until the gates are green. This document only resolves what was
placeholder before: the theme (caregiving and the promise kept), the magnet (Quick
Toolkit), the second product (the full Toolkit), and the pitch lanes.
