To Tesla, Inc. Resolutions:

I am the purchaser of a new 2025 Tesla Model Y Long Range All-Wheel Drive under
Order No. RN125485754 (order accepted August 12, 2025; delivered [DELIVERY
DATE]; VIN [VIN]). I am sending this written notice pursuant to the dispute
provision of my Motor Vehicle Order Agreement.

The IRS has disallowed the $7,500 New Clean Vehicle Credit (IRC § 30D) on my
2025 return. In Notice CP23 dated June 8, 2026, the IRS states the credit was
disallowed because **the VIN reported on my return did not match IRS records.**
Under § 30D, Tesla — as the seller — was required to submit a time-of-sale
report for my vehicle through the IRS Energy Credits Online (ECO) portal. A VIN
mismatch points to an error or omission in that seller filing.

When I asked your representatives about this, I received three different and
inconsistent explanations (that my income was too high; that "Head of Household"
was entered during the transaction; and that a new employee may have entered the
information incorrectly). I was not permitted to photograph or keep a copy of the
internal document said to explain the discrepancy. I am therefore making the
following formal requests in writing:

**1. Provide a copy of the time-of-sale report (seller report)** Tesla submitted
to the IRS Energy Credits Online portal for my vehicle, including:
   - the exact VIN Tesla reported;
   - the buyer name, filing status, and any income/MAGI attestation reported;
   - the date of sale reported; and
   - the IRS ECO confirmation or acceptance status for that submission.

**2. Confirm in writing the exact VIN** Tesla transmitted to the IRS for this
sale, so I can compare it against the VIN on my vehicle, my Final Price Sheet,
and my Form 8936.

**3. If the report was never accepted, was filed late, or contained an incorrect
VIN, filing status, or other error, submit a corrected time-of-sale report**
through the IRS Energy Credits Online portal for this VIN, and confirm to me when
the correction has been accepted.

For the record: I qualify for the credit. I filed Married Filing Jointly with
modified AGI below the $300,000 threshold, the Model Y is under the $80,000 SUV
MSRP cap, and the vehicle was purchased for personal use. The only obstacle is
the VIN-reporting discrepancy in the seller's filing, which is within Tesla's
control to correct.

This matter is **time-sensitive**: the IRS notice sets a disagreement deadline of
**August 7, 2026**, and interest is accruing on the assessed amount. Please
respond no later than **[DATE — suggest ~14 days out]** so I can resolve this
before the IRS deadline.

I am also entitled to a copy of the seller report under the clean vehicle credit
rules, and I ask that you provide it rather than treat it as internal-only
information.
