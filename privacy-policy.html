The Cardinal's Toolkit

Privacy Policy

Last updated: July 2026

The Cardinal's Toolkit is built to keep your caregiving notes private. The app has no account, collects no personal information, and sends nothing off your device. What you write stays with you.

What we collect

Nothing. The app does not collect, transmit, sell, or share any personal information. There is no sign-up and no user account.

What the app stores, and where

Everything you enter — journal entries, mood check-ins, checklist progress, and your medication list — is saved only on your device, using the phone's own storage. It is not uploaded to us or to anyone else, because there is no server and no cloud account behind the app.

If you delete the app, that information is removed with it. Because the data lives only on your device, we cannot recover it for you — which is why the app lets you export a backup you control (below).

Sharing and export are yours to choose

The app includes buttons to share a checklist or your medication list, and to export a backup of your data. These use your device's standard share sheet: you choose whether to send that information, and to whom (for example, saving to Files, emailing it, or printing it). The app never sends anything on its own.

No tracking, no analytics, no ads

The app contains no analytics, no advertising, no third-party tracking software, and no marketing SDKs. We do not build a profile of you and have no data to sell because we never receive any.

Links to phone numbers and websites

The Resources section provides phone numbers and links to outside organizations (such as Medicare, 988, and state aging services). Tapping them opens your phone, browser, or another app. Those organizations have their own privacy practices, which this policy does not cover.

Children

The app is intended for adult family caregivers and is not directed to children under 13.

Not medical or legal advice

The Cardinal's Toolkit offers organization and self-care support. It is not a medical, legal, or financial service, and it does not diagnose, treat, or advise. For those decisions, rely on qualified professionals.

Changes to this policy

If the app ever changes how it handles information, this page will be updated with a new date above. Because the app currently collects nothing, we expect changes to be rare.
