# Note to Dean Ferrera — his place in the book + blessing to keep his name

Dean —

I've written a book. It took me a couple of years, and it's the truest thing
I've ever done. It's called *The Cardinal's Promise*. On the surface it's about
my dad, Lou — the man who chose me, drove a U-Haul to West Palm Beach to pull me
out of the worst of it, and never once made me feel I had to earn my place. Under
that it's about addiction and getting sober, about the seventeen years I've
strung together one day at a time, and about the work I do now, sitting with
families in hospice when they're losing the person they love most. Fifteen days
after my dad died, a cardinal landed on my car mirror and wouldn't leave. That's
where the title comes from.

I'm telling you because you're in it, and I wouldn't put your name on a page
without your okay.

You come in the way you came into my life — early, before I'd even set foot on
campus. That party in Jersey, the mixer for kids heading to Miami, and I walked
out of it already knowing a few faces, yours the main one. I write that you
became one of my best friends, because you did. You're there for the MTV night in
Cancun, too — the hotel phone rings, you answer it, and you hand it to me with a
look on your face I'd never seen before. *It's MTV.* And you're in the senior-year
condo we destroyed, the four of us about to scatter into our real lives.

That's it. Warm, true, and only what you'd tell anyone yourself. I don't mention
your work or anything private — just the friend you were and are.

If you're good with it, I'll keep your name exactly as it is. If you'd rather I
change it or pull it, say the word and it's done, no hard feelings — you'll still
be one of my best friends whether or not you're on the page.

Either way, I'd love for you to read it before it goes out. It would mean a lot to
have your eyes on it.

Love you, brother.

Rob
