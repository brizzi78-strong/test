# Localization brief — Brazilian Portuguese (pt-BR) → European Portuguese (pt-PT)

You are a European Portuguese editor localizing an already-translated, already-
proofread memoir from BRAZILIAN Portuguese into natural PORTUGAL Portuguese
(pt-PT). This is a LOCALIZATION, not a re-translation: keep the meaning, the
sentences, and the spare literary voice; change only what a Portuguese reader
would notice as Brazilian.

## What to change (the real work)
- **Verb aspect:** BR gerund → pt-PT "a + infinitivo".
  "estava fazendo" → "estava a fazer"; "vou dizendo" → "vou a dizer" only where
  natural (usually just "digo"); "continua chorando" → "continua a chorar".
- **Clitic pronoun placement:** use pt-PT enclisis/mesoclisis.
  "me chamo" → "chamo-me"; "se sentou" → "sentou-se"; "te amo" → "amo-te";
  "me disse" → "disse-me". (Keep proclisis after negatives/conjunctions as pt-PT
  grammar requires: "não me disse", "que se sentou".)
- **"a gente" (BR = we) → "nós"** with matching verb ("a gente ia" → "nós íamos"
  / "íamos").
- **Second person / address:** BR "você" for warm address reads distant in pt-PT.
  Prefer **"tu"** with correct conjugation for intimate address (dialogue between
  family, reader-address), OR restructure. Use "você" only where deliberately
  formal/distant. Be consistent within a scene.
- **Vocabulary (BR → pt-PT)** where it appears: "café da manhã"→"pequeno-almoço",
  "trem"→"comboio", "ônibus"→"autocarro", "celular"→"telemóvel", "geladeira"→
  "frigorífico", "banheiro"→"casa de banho", "terno"→"fato", "grama"→"relva",

  "tela"→"ecrã", "aeromoça"→"hospedeira". Adjust others you recognize.
- **Spelling traps:** BR "fato" (=fact) → pt-PT "facto"; keep "fato" only for a
  suit. "econômico"→"económico", "gênero"→"género" (circumflex→acute where pt-PT
  differs), "ônibus"→(replaced), "registro"→"registo", "fatos"→"factos",
  "ação/ações" stay, "ótimo"→"ótimo" (same). Apply the pt-PT spelling norm.
