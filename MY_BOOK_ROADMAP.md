# My Book Roadmap — Memoir, Finished Manuscript, Zero Audience

The [thesis](BOOK_SALES_THESIS.md) applied to this specific situation. Profile:

| Fact | Implication |
|---|---|
| **Memoir / personal story** | Sells on the story's hook and theme, not the author's credentials. The natural discovery channel is *you telling the story out loud* — podcasts, short-form video. This is the Goggins path. |
| **Finished manuscript** | Content validation (thesis factor 2) can't shape the writing anymore — so it shifts to **packaging**: which parts of the story you lead with, the title, the theme. Those are still fully testable. |
| **Zero audience** | Gate G1 can't be met with an owned list in a reasonable time. We meet it the borrowed-audience way (Ferriss/Goggins): a booked slate of other people's audiences, while a small email list grows as a by-product. |
| **5–10 hrs/week** | Enough for the two workstreams that matter — weekly story content + weekly pitching — with nothing left over. Everything not on the schedule below gets cut, not squeezed in. |

**Realistic timeline: ~6 months to launch.** Launching sooner means launching to nobody —
the thesis says that money and effort spent then is mostly wasted. The manuscript being
done is an advantage: all 5–10 hours go to audience and packaging, none to writing.

**Do not set a launch date now.** The launch date is set the week the
[gate tracker](#gate-tracker) goes green.

## The one decision that comes first: the theme

Nobody buys "the story of my life." Readers buy memoirs by **theme** — the universal
thing your story is *about*: resilience, recovery, reinvention, grief, an against-the-odds
bet, escaping something. *Can't Hurt Me* isn't sold as "David Goggins's autobiography";
it's sold as "master your mind" with his life as proof.

Week-one homework, before anything else:

1. Write the story's hook in one sentence, in the form: *"What happens when `<person in
   situation>` `<does/faces the extraordinary thing>` — and what it teaches about
   `<universal theme>`."*
2. Write three different versions leading with three different episodes from the book.
3. Find 3–5 comp memoirs (last ~5 years) with the same *theme* — not the same life.
   Their categories, covers, and podcast trails become the map for everything below.

You will not guess which hook is right — Phase 1 tests them in public (that's the
Ferriss title-test, adapted).

## Phase 0 — Setup (weeks 1–4)

- [ ] Theme + 3 hook variants written (above)
- [ ] Comps identified; note which podcasts each comp author appeared on
      (search "`<comp author>` podcast interview" — that list is your pitch slate)
- [ ] Email list live with a reader magnet: the strongest single chapter or scene as a
      free excerpt ("Read the chapter where `<hook moment>` — free")
- [ ] One-page author site: hook, excerpt signup, about, contact
- [ ] Pick **one** short-form platform for story content — for memoir this is almost
      always TikTok or Instagram Reels (story-to-camera), or Substack essays if you're
      much stronger in writing than on camera. One platform. Not two.
- [ ] Draft your podcast one-sheet: who you are in two lines, the hook, 5 questions
      you're great on, links. (You'll revise it after Phase 1 data.)

## Phase 1 — Validate the hook in public (months 2–3)

The Manson/Ferriss move, adapted for a finished memoir: **the market picks the packaging.**

- **Publish 2 story pieces per week** on the chosen platform. Each piece = one episode
  or moment from the book, told directly (60–90s video or short essay). Rotate the
  three hook framings across posts.
- **Measure:** which framing/episodes get saves, shares, comments, follows — not just
  views. After ~16–20 pieces you'll know which version of the story strangers respond to.
- **That winner becomes:** the title/subtitle direction, the cover brief, the blurb's
  first line, the podcast pitch, and the excerpt you give away.
- Meanwhile, **start the podcast ladder at the bottom:** pitch 3 small shows/week
  (under ~5k downloads/episode, in your theme's niche — recovery shows, resilience
  shows, whatever matches). Small hosts say yes fast; each appearance is practice
  telling the story tight, and a recorded credit for pitching upward.
- End-of-phase output: validated hook, 3–5 recorded small-show appearances, first
  few hundred email subscribers, one platform with proof of resonance.

## Phase 2 — Package and book the slate (months 4–5)

- [ ] Title/subtitle finalized from Phase 1 data; cover commissioned to match the
      comps' shelf (memoir covers signal theme — check your comps side by side)
- [ ] Blurb written per [plan §2](BOOK_MARKETING_PLAN.md): first line = the winning hook
- [ ] Editing pass if not done (memoir minimum: professional copyedit)
- [ ] Keywords/categories from the comps ([plan §2](BOOK_MARKETING_PLAN.md))
- [ ] **Pitch the mid-tier slate:** 10–15 podcasts in the 5k–50k range, using Phase 1
      appearances as credits and the validated hook as the subject line. Ask for
      launch-window air dates (they book 4–8 weeks out — this is why Phase 2 starts now).
- [ ] Recruit the ARC team (target 25–40): platform followers + email list +
      BookSirens/StoryOrigin memoir readers. ARCs out 3–4 weeks before launch.
- [ ] Book the promo stack for launch week ([plan §7](BOOK_MARKETING_PLAN.md))

## Phase 3 — Launch (month ~6, gated)

Run [plan §6 launch week](BOOK_MARKETING_PLAN.md) as written — $0.99 launch price,
KDP Select, stacked promos — with the earned-media layer on top:

- Podcast episodes airing across launch week ± 2 weeks (this is the centerpiece)
- Every episode's call-to-action: the book at $0.99 *this week* + the free-excerpt link
  (captures listeners who don't buy today onto the list — they're next promo's buyers)
- ARC team posts reviews day 1; email list gets the announcement day 1
- Keep pitching bigger shows *after* launch with launch results as the credit —
  Goggins's Rogan moment came via appearances that postdated the book. The tour
  doesn't end at launch; the book makes you *more* bookable.

## The weekly schedule (5–10 hours)

| Block | Hours | What |
|---|---|---|
| Story content | 3–4 | Write/record + publish 2 pieces; note what resonated |
| Pitching | 2–3 | 3 podcast pitches + follow-ups on last week's |
| List + community | 1 | Email the list (even at 50 subscribers); reply to every comment/DM |
| Review metrics | ½ | Update the gate tracker below; adjust next week's hooks |

If a week only has 5 hours: content and pitching happen, the rest slides. Those two
compound; nothing else does.

## Gate Tracker

Launch date gets set when this table is green. Update it weekly (the ½-hour block).

| Gate | Target (adapted for this profile) | Current | Status |
|---|---|---|---|
| G1 — audience trust | 1,000+ engaged email subs **and** booked slate reaching 50k+ target listeners in launch window | 0 | 🔴 |
| G2 — validated packaging | One hook framing clearly outperforms across 16+ published pieces; title/cover/blurb derived from it | not started | 🔴 |
| G3 — discovery slate | 10+ appearances booked to air in launch window (mid-tier counts; one big show can cover half the reach target) | 0 | 🔴 |
| G4 — ignition team | 25+ ARC readers committed to day-1 review | 0 | 🔴 |
| G5 — word-of-mouth check | Beta/ARC readers recommending it unprompted; excerpt page converts visitors to signups at ≥ 25% | unknown | 🔴 |

*(G1 is set lower than the thesis default because the borrowed-audience path
substitutes slate reach for list size — the Goggins/Ferriss adjustment.)*

## What NOT to do (this profile's specific traps)

- **Don't publish now "just to have it out."** A memoir that launches to zero audience
  gets zero reviews and a dead sales rank — and relaunching a stale book is harder
  than launching a fresh one. The manuscript loses nothing by waiting 6 months.
- **Don't buy ads.** Zero audience + memoir + one book = the exact case where the
  thesis says paid reach burns money ([plan §8](BOOK_MARKETING_PLAN.md) prerequisites
  aren't close to met).
- **Don't tell the whole story in content.** Each piece is one scene with a pull toward
  the rest — the book is the payoff, content is the trailer.
- **Don't spread across platforms.** One content platform + podcasts. That's it, at
  5–10 hrs/week.
- **Don't skip the theme work.** Every hour spent before the hook is validated is
  spent guessing.
