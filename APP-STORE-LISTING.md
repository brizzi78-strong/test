## Description (max 4000)

Caring for an aging parent can arrive all at once — a fall, a diagnosis, a
phone call — and suddenly you're the one making decisions you were never
trained for. The Cardinal's Toolkit is a calm, private companion for exactly
those days.

It's the app that goes with the caregiver handbook *The Cardinal's Toolkit* by
Rob Brizzi, CDP. Its checklists, phone numbers, and guidance come straight from
the book — organized so you can find the next right step without reading a
whole chapter.

WHAT'S INSIDE

• Crisis checklists, color-coded by urgency — red for "act today," purple for
  "watch closely," blue for "plan ahead." Start with "In the First 24 Hours":
  seven actions, not seven chapters.

• A real medication list — type each medicine (name, dose, when, why, who
  prescribed it) and hand your phone to the ER or a new doctor. Bring the list,
  not just the bottles.

• Gentle tools for hard moments — a guided minute of breathing, the 5-4-3-2-1
  grounding exercise, and affirmations written for caregiving's heaviest days.

• A private journal and a daily heart check-in, so you can notice how you're
  really doing across the week.

• Trusted phone numbers — 988, the Eldercare Locator, the Alzheimer's
  Association 24/7 line, Medicare, veteran caregiver support, and North
  Carolina resources like SHIIP and Project C.A.R.E.

• Share and back up — send a checklist to a sibling, or export a plain-text
  backup of everything, so a lost phone never means losing your parent's
  record.

PRIVATE BY DESIGN

There is no account and no sign-up. Everything you enter — journal, mood,
checklists, medications — stays on your device. Nothing is uploaded, nothing is
tracked, and there are no ads. When you share or export, you choose where it
goes; the app never sends anything on its own.

A NOTE OF CARE

The Cardinal's Toolkit offers organization and self-care support. It is not
medical, legal, or financial advice — for those decisions, lean on your
parent's doctors, an elder law attorney, and the professionals in the app's
Resources tab. And if caregiving is overwhelming you, that page has people to
call who understand.

You are not alone. You are the difference.
