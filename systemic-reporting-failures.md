# Systemic Dealer Reporting Failures — Supporting Research

Purpose: document that "information entered but not correctly received/filed" in
the clean-vehicle credit system is a **known, nationwide, IRS-acknowledged**
dealer-side failure — not a buyer error. Supports (a) the small-claims argument
that Tesla's app/systems are not foolproof and the report should have been filed,
and (b) the IRS CP23 response.

## Key findings (sourced)

1. **Nationwide pattern.** Buyers across the country were denied the credit
   "because their dealers did not report the sale" through IRS Energy Credits
   Online. NPR spoke to "a half-dozen other EV buyers throughout the country" with
   the same problem. (NPR, "EV tax credit denied for some car buyers," 02/28/2025;
   OPB reprint.)

2. **A dealer GM admitted the error (parallel case).** In the Kristina Meier case,
   the dealership's **general manager conceded** his employees "just used the
   paperwork that was loaded into their dealership computer systems. 'It was the
   wrong form.'" → Direct precedent that dealer data-entry/forms fail and the
   fault is the dealer's, not the buyer's.

3. **IRS acknowledged it and reopened the portal.** The IRS reinstated/reopened
   Energy Credits Online for dealers to submit time-of-sale reports retroactively,
   and waived the 72-hour deadline. (CBM CPA, 04/01/2025; NADA.)

4. **IRS-stated buyer remedy.** Per an IRS spokesperson (NPR): *"taxpayers who
   receive an error or rejection of their Clean Vehicle Tax Credit submission can
   submit their proof of purchase of a qualified vehicle directly to the IRS."*
   → The Brizzis hold that proof: the signed Form 15400 + purchase agreement.

## How to use it

- **Small claims (July 22):** Tesla's app entry is not proof of buyer error — this
  same failure (dealer forms/systems not correctly received) is nationwide and the
  IRS itself acknowledged it. The seller's duty to file is independent of income.
- **IRS CP23 response:** invoke the IRS's own remedy — submit proof of purchase
  (signed Form 15400 + purchase agreement) directly and request the credit be
  allowed on that proof.
- **Caveat:** these are news-reported examples, not binding precedent; use as
  pattern/credibility support.
